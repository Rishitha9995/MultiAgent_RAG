Construct a timeline for the coronavirus outbreak.
Here's a timeline of major events during the coronavirus outbreak:

December 31, 2019 - China alerts the World Health Organization (WHO) of several flu-like cases with an unknown cause in Wuhan, Hubei Province.

January 7, 2020 - Chinese authorities identify the new virus as belonging to the coronavirus family.

January 11, 2020 - China reports its first death from the virus.

January 13, 2020 - Thailand reports the first case outside China.

January 20, 2020 - The first cases are reported outside of Asia, in the United States and Australia.

January 23, 2020 - Wuhan is placed under quarantine, halting all travel in and out of the city.

January 30, 2020 - WHO declares the outbreak a global emergency.

February 2, 2020 - The first death outside China is reported in the Philippines.

February 11, 2020 - The virus is officially named COVID-19.

March 11, 2020 - WHO declares COVID-19 a pandemic.

March 13, 2020 - Many countries begin implementing travel restrictions, quarantines, and lockdowns.

March 16, 2020 - Global stock markets suffer significant losses.

March 24, 2020 - The 2020 Olympics scheduled to take place in Tokyo are postponed until 2021.

April 2, 2020 - The global number of confirmed cases surpassed 1 million.

April 7, 2020 - The global death toll reached 100,000.

June 2020 - The number of COVID-19 cases in India and Brazil begins to spike.

July 2020 - The United States becomes the first country to reach 3 million cases.

August 2020 - Russia announces it has developed the first vaccine for COVID-19.

December 8, 2020 - The UK begins its vaccination program.

December 11, 2020 - The Pfizer/BioNTech COVID-19 vaccine is authorized for emergency use in the US.

January 6, 2021 - The global death toll reaches 2 million.

February 17, 2021 - The Johnson & Johnson COVID-19 vaccine is authorized for emergency use by the US.

March 11, 2021 - The WHO reports that more than 300 million vaccine doses have been administered worldwide.

April 2021 - India experiences a devastating second wave of COVID-19
