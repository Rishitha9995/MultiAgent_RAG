Create a ‘nice-to-have’ list for a product inventory system.
Product inventory systems vary depending on the industry, type of business, products, and specific needs. However, here is a list of some ‘nice-to-have’ features that can enhance the functionality of any product inventory system:

1. Real-Time Tracking and Reporting: A system that provides real-time tracking of the stock levels, sales, and orders so the business can make data-driven decisions.

2. Mobile Accessibility: Allows inventory management on-the-go through a mobile app, which can provide more flexibility to the business.

3. Automated Reordering: A system that triggers reordering when stock levels reach a certain point, reducing the chances of running out of stock.

4. Barcoding and Scanning: The ability to use barcode scanning for faster and more accurate inventory management.

5. Multichannel Integration: Integrates with multiple sales channels, such as e-commerce, brick-and-mortar, and other marketplaces, to provide a centralized platform for inventory management.

6. Forecasting: Uses data analytics to provide forecasting of future demand and sales, allowing businesses to plan ahead and make informed decisions.

7. Batch and Expiration Tracking: For businesses dealing with perishable goods, this feature helps to track and manage products based on their batch numbers and expiration dates.

8. Custom Alerts: Sends alerts for low stock levels, pending orders, and any discrepancies, allowing businesses to take timely action.

9. User Permissions: Allows the business to set up different permission levels for different users to access and manage inventory.

10. Inventory History: Keeps a record of past inventory levels, sales, and orders, providing businesses with valuable insights and the ability to track trends over time.

In summary, these features can enhance the functionality of a product inventory system, making it more efficient, user-friendly, and data-driven.
