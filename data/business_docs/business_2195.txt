Give an example of a potential problem that can occur if a certain policy is implemented.
One potential problem that can occur if a policy to incentivize companies to reduce air pollution is implemented, is the possibility of companies exploiting loopholes in the policy to receive the incentives without actually reducing pollution. For instance, a company could partake in a practice known as “emissions leakage,” where they move their high-polluting operations to a different location, thereby appearing to reduce emissions in the incentivized area but not actually reducing their overall emissions. This could lead to the policy being less effective in achieving its goal of reducing air pollution, as some companies may prioritize their profits over the interests of the environment.
