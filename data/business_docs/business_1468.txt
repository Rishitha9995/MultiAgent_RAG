Pick the main issues facing the global economy.
Some of the main challenges currently facing the global economy include the following:

1. Effects of the COVID-19 pandemic: The pandemic has caused widespread disruption to global trade and mobility, as well as reduced demand for goods and services, which has led to negative economic growth, increased unemployment and rising debt levels.

2. Wealth and income inequality: Income and wealth disparities are widening globally with a large portion of the population experiencing stagnant wages and limited access to opportunities while a small proportion continues to accumulate immense wealth.

3. Geopolitical tensions and trade conflicts: Tensions between major economies, particularly the US and China, as well as Brexit and other disagreements, have led to an unpredictable trade environment and uncertainty in the global economy.

4. Climate Change: Environmental degradation, resource depletion, and climate change pose a significant threat to the global economy in terms of damage to infrastructure, reduced productivity, increased costs and dangers to human life.

5. Aging populations: In many developed countries, the population is aging rapidly, putting a strain on social security and healthcare systems, as well as reducing the size of the active workforce.

6. Technological disruption: Rapid advancements in technology are disrupting traditional industries and labor markets, leading to job losses and the need for re-skilling on a large scale.

7. Debt levels: Many countries and households are grappling with high levels of debt, which limits their ability to invest in growth opportunities and increases their vulnerability to economic shocks.
