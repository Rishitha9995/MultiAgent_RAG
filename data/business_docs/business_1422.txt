What is the difference between a formal and informal letter?
A formal letter is a type of written communication that follows a structured format, like business letters or letters requesting information or making a complaint. These letters usually use formal language, a professional tone and proper salutations, such as “Dear Sir/Madam”. They follow a standard set of conventions, including a heading, date, and address, formal greeting, introduction, body, conclusion, and closing. They're usually written to convey official, professional or legal matters.

On the other hand, an informal letter is a type of written communication used for personal or casual purposes, like writing to a friend, family member or acquaintance. The language used is usually more colloquial and casual, the tone is friendly, and the format is more relaxed. Informal letters typically do not follow the same structured format as formal letters and may include personal anecdotes, emotions, or casual expressions that reflect the relationship between the writer and the recipient. They are used to convey personal matters or share news, opinions or feelings.
