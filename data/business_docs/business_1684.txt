Tell me why a banker would calculate loan interest rates.
A banker would calculate loan interest rates for a number of reasons. Firstly, interest rates are a major aspect of the loan and it is important for the banker to determine a fair and competitive interest rate that can attract borrowers. Additionally, the interest rate is the cost of borrowing money, and the income for the bank, so calculating it helps to determine how much the bank will earn from the loan. This is important in assessing the profitability and viability of the loan for the bank.

Furthermore, the interest rate is also used to determine the borrower's monthly repayment amount. The banker needs to calculate and reveal the interest rate as a part of informing the borrowers of the terms of the loan, allowing them to make informed decisions on whether or not they can afford the repayments.

Finally, the interest rate is influenced by various factors such as the creditworthiness of the borrower, market conditions and the level of risk associated with the loan. The banker needs to take all these factors into account when calculating the interest rate to ensure it is fair and appropriate to the specific loan and borrower.
