Describe how solar energy works in simple terms
Solar energy is energy harnessed from the sun. It is a renewable resource, meaning it cannot be used up, unlike fossil fuels. Here is how solar energy works in simple terms:

The sun emits energy in the form of light and heat. This energy can be captured and turned into electricity through the use of solar panels. Solar panels are made of photovoltaic (PV) cells, which are specially designed to capture sunlight and convert it into electricity.

When sunlight hits a PV cell, it is absorbed and creates an electric current. The PV cells are wired together into a panel, so the individual currents combine to create a larger flow of electricity. This electricity can then be harnessed to power homes or businesses.

Solar energy can also be used to heat water, through the use of solar thermal systems. These systems work by absorbing the sun's heat and using it to warm up water that is stored in a tank.

In summary, solar energy is created when the sun's energy is absorbed by solar panels or thermal systems and then converted into usable electricity or heat. This clean, renewable energy source is becoming more and more popular as people look for ways to reduce their carbon footprint and save on energy costs.
