Provide a use case of reinforcement learning.
Reinforcement learning is a subset of machine learning that involves an agent interacting with its environment to understand the best possible action to take in order to achieve a goal. One major use case of reinforcement learning is in the field of gaming, with popular examples such as AlphaGo and OpenAI Five, both of which have defeated human masters in the games Go and Dota2, respectively.

Here, the reinforcement learning agent or system is designed to perform actions within the game environment, observe the resulting changes in the game state, and understand whether the action led it closer to victory or defeat, based on the reward signal provided by the environment. This reward signal informs the agent whether the action taken was good or bad, allowing the agent to adjust its strategy accordingly. Over time and through many iterations of gameplay, the reinforcement learning agent learns the best possible actions to take to reach its goal of winning the game, by consistently choosing actions that lead it to accumulate the highest reward signals.

Reinforcement learning has potential use cases in other fields as well, such as robotics, healthcare, finance, and transportation, where learning how to make decisions based on environmental feedback can be key to achieving desired outcomes.
