Give two reasons why we should abort a project.
1. Unanticipated challenges and roadblocks making it no longer viable: Sometimes, upon further examination and exploration, a project that initially appeared promising may face insurmountable obstacles or unanticipated challenges which affect the project's feasibility or achieve the previously set goals. Continuing with the project despite these challenges could result in wasted resources and time, so it may be best to abort the project to prevent further loss.

2. Changes in business priorities or external circumstances: A project may no longer align with an organization's strategic direction or its business goals may have changed, rendering the project less important or obsolete. Additionally, external circumstances such as economic changes, natural disasters or a global crisis can significantly impact the project's outcome. In such cases, it is prudent to abort the project and reallocate resources to more pressing priorities.
