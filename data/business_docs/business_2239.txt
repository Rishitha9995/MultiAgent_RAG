Generate a timeline for the US in the 20th century.
Here is a timeline of major events in the United States during the 20th century:

- 1901: William McKinley, the 25th President of the United States, is assassinated. Theodore Roosevelt becomes the 26th President of the United States.
- 1903: The Wright Brothers make the first powered flight in Kitty Hawk, North Carolina.
- 1914-1918: World War I takes place. The United States enters the war in 1917.
- 1919: The Treaty of Versailles formally ends World War I. The 18th Amendment is ratified, prohibiting the sale, manufacture, and transportation of alcohol.
- 1920: The 19th Amendment to the United States Constitution, granting women the right to vote, is ratified.
- 1929: The stock market crashes, marking the beginning of the Great Depression.
- 1933: Franklin Delano Roosevelt is inaugurated as the 32nd President of the United States. He introduces the New Deal to combat the Great Depression.
- 1941: The United States enters World War II following the bombing of Pearl Harbor by Japan.
- 1945: World War II ends with the surrender of Germany and Japan.
- 1947: The Cold War between the United States and the Soviet Union begins.
- 1950-1953: The Korean War takes place.
- 1954: The Supreme Court rules in Brown v. Board of Education that segregated schools are unconstitutional.
- 1963: President John F. Kennedy is assassinated.
- 1964: The Civil Rights Act is signed into law, outlawing discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, or national origin.
- 1969: Neil Armstrong becomes the first man to walk on the moon.
- 1974: President Richard Nixon resigns following the Watergate scandal.
- 1980: Ronald Reagan is elected as the 40th President of the United States.
- 1989: The Berlin Wall falls, marking the end of the Cold War.
- 1991: The Persian Gulf War takes place.
- 1998: The impeachment of President Bill Clinton takes place.
- 1999: The United States hands over control of the Panama Canal to Panama.
