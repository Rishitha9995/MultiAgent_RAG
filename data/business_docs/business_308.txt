Describe the process of hydropower generation in a few sentences.
Hydropower generation, also known as hydroelectric power, is the process of harnessing the energy of moving water to generate electricity. Typically, water is stored in a dam or reservoir, and then released to flow through a turbine, which rotates and powers an electric generator. The energy generated is then sent through power lines to homes and businesses. The amount of electricity generated depends on the size of the turbine and the volume and speed of the water flow. It is a renewable and sustainable source of energy because it relies on the water cycle and rainfall to replenish the water supply used for generation.
