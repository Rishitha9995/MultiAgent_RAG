Explain why recycling is important.
Recycling is important for a number of reasons. Firstly, it helps to conserve natural resources such as timber, water, and minerals by reducing the need to extract and refine new materials. This means that non-renewable resources, like fossil fuels, can be saved and used for longer periods of time. 

Secondly, recycling also reduces the amount of waste that ends up in landfills or is incinerated, resulting in lower levels of pollution and carbon emissions. Landfills take up space and can release pollutants into the air, soil, and groundwater while incineration releases carbon dioxide and other harmful gases into the atmosphere.

Thirdly, recycling can also save energy. It often takes less energy to recycle materials than it does to create new ones from scratch. For example, recycling aluminum cans saves up to 95% of the energy needed to produce new ones.

Finally, recycling helps boost the economy by creating jobs in the recycling and manufacturing industries. It also reduces the cost of waste disposal and can help communities save money on waste management.

In summary, recycling is important because it conserves natural resources, reduces pollution, saves energy, and creates jobs. It is a vital part of maintaining a sustainable, healthy planet for future generations.
