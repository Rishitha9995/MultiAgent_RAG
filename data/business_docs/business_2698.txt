Describe the current economic situation of the given country.
Japan, the world's third-largest economy, is currently experiencing a period of moderate growth and is in recovery from the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic. The pandemic had caused a sharp contraction in the economy, with a decrease in exports, consumer spending, and industrial production. In response, the government and the Bank of Japan implemented large scale stimulus measures, including the expansion of its monetary easing program and fiscal measures to support businesses and households.

Recent reports suggest that the Japanese economy is gradually improving, with increases in industrial production, retail sales, and private consumption. The unemployment rate has also been relatively low. However, despite the positive developments, the recovery is still fragile and uncertain, with risks arising from possible fresh outbreaks of COVID-19 and the slow pace of vaccination rollout. Moreover, the country is still facing long-term structural issues such as an aging population, high public debt, and low inflation.
