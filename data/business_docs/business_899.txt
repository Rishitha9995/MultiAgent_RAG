Explain the pros and cons of the Agile methodology.
Agile methodology is a popular approach to project management and software development that focuses on flexibility, collaboration and customer satisfaction. Like any approach, there are pros and cons to using this methodology.

Pros:

- Flexibility: Agile methodology allows for changes to be made to the project throughout the development process, which means it can adapt quickly to changes in requirements or priorities.

- Continuous Feedback: Agile methodology emphasizes collaboration and constant communication between the team members and the client, leading to a continuous feedback loop that allows for quick changes and improvements to the project.

- Customer Focus: Agile methodology puts the customer at the center of the development process, leading to a better understanding of their needs and resulting in a more satisfactory final product.

- Early and Incremental Delivery: Agile methodology allows for the delivery of smaller, incremental pieces of the product along the way, leading to early and continuous delivery of valuable software.

Cons:

- Lack of Documentation: Due to the focus on speed and flexibility, Agile methodology can sometimes lead to a lack of documentation which can cause problems in the long run.

- Difficult to Predict: Due to the flexibility and adaptability of Agile methodology, it can be difficult to accurately predict the timeline and budget for a project.

- Not Suitable for All Projects: Agile methodology works best for projects with a high level of uncertainty or changing requirements. For projects with well-defined requirements and a set timeline, other methodologies may be more suitable.

- Requires High level of Customer Involvement: Agile methodology requires a high level of customer involvement throughout the development process. If the customer is not able or willing to commit to this level of involvement, it can hinder the success of the project.
