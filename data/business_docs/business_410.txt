What type of expenses should be included on an income statement?
An income statement typically includes the following types of expenses:

1. Cost of Goods Sold (COGS): The direct expenses associated with producing or purchasing goods or services that were sold during the period, such as raw materials, direct labor, and manufacturing overhead.

2. Operating Expenses: The general and administrative costs associated with running the business, such as rent, utilities, insurance, salaries, and office supplies.

3. Selling Expenses: Costs associated with promoting and selling products or services, such as advertising, sales commissions, and travel expenses.

4. Depreciation and Amortization: The systematic allocation of the cost of long-term assets, such as buildings, equipment, and intangible assets, over their useful lives.

5. Interest Expense: The cost of borrowing money or using credit.

6. Taxes: The amount of income tax the business owes on its earnings during the period.

7. Extraordinary Expenses: Unusual or nonrecurring expenses that are not part of the business's normal operations, such as natural disaster damages or lawsuit settlements.

It's important to note that the types of expenses included on an income statement may vary depending on the nature of the business and its industry.
