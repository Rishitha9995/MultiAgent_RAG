Construct an itinerary for a tourist visiting London, England.
Day 1:
Morning - Arrive in London and check in to your hotel. Take a leisurely stroll around the city to familiarize yourself with your surroundings and shake off the jet lag.
Afternoon - Visit the British Museum, one of the most famous museums in the world, to see its vast collection of ancient artifacts, art, and antiquities.
Evening - Experience the bustling energy of Piccadilly Circus, and enjoy dinner at one of the many excellent local restaurants.

Day 2:
Morning - Head to Buckingham Palace to witness the Changing of the Guard ceremony, and then take a tour of the palace, the official residence of the British monarch.
Afternoon - Cross the River Thames to visit the Tower of London, a fortress that has served as a palace, prison, and treasury over the centuries, and take a tour of the Crown Jewels.
Evening - Take a sunset ride on the London Eye, a giant Ferris wheel on the South Bank that offers panoramic views of the city.

Day 3:
Morning - Wander through the beautiful gardens of Regent’s Park, and visit the London Zoo.
Afternoon - Explore the vibrant Camden Market, where you can find everything from vintage clothing to street food from all over the world.
Evening - Catch a show in the West End, London’s theatre district, and have dinner at one of the area’s great restaurants.

Day 4:
Morning - Visit the Tate Modern, one of the most popular modern art museums in the world, housed in a massive converted power station on the South Bank.
Afternoon - Take a walk across the Millennium Bridge, and visit St. Paul’s Cathedral, an iconic London landmark and a masterpiece of Baroque architecture.
Evening - Head to Covent Garden, a popular shopping and dining destination, and enjoy a meal and drinks at one of the area’s many great eateries.

Day 5:
Morning - Take a day trip to Stonehenge, one of the most famous prehistoric monuments in the world, located about two hours from London by train or bus.
Afternoon - Return to London and visit the natural History Museum to see its impressive collection of dinosaur skeletons and other natural wonders.
Evening - Visit the lively Shoreditch neighborhood, known for its street art, vintage shops, and trendy bars and restaurants.

Day 6:
Morning - Visit Westminster Abbey, a magnificent Gothic church and the site of numerous royal weddings and coronations over the centuries.
