Explain the phrase "money is a good servant but a bad master".
The saying "money is a good servant but a bad master" conveys the idea that money can be incredibly useful and helpful when it is handled responsibly and used in a way that benefits our lives. When we manage our finances wisely, money can provide for our needs, give us security, and allow us to enjoy some of life's pleasures. In this sense, money serves us seamlessly and can even make our lives easier.

However, if we allow money to become our primary focus and let it control our actions and decisions, then it can become a bad master. When the pursuit of money takes over our lives, it can lead to greed, stress, and a host of other negative outcomes. In such a situation, we are no longer in control of our finances, but rather, our finances control us.

In essence, the phrase "money is a good servant but a bad master" is a reminder to use money as a tool to improve our lives but to be careful not to let it take control over us.
