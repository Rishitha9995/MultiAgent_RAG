Find the definition of the following financial term
A stock split is a corporate action in which a company increases the number of its outstanding shares by issuing more shares to its existing shareholders. A stock split causes a reduction in the price per share, making the stock more accessible to smaller investors. The total value of the shares an investor holds remains the same, as the proportionate ownership in the company doesn't change. A company may decide to perform a stock split for numerous reasons, including to make the stock look more attractive to investors or to increase the liquidity of the shares in the market. Common stock split ratios include 2-for-1 or 3-for-1, which means that the shareholder will receive two or three shares for every share they currently own.
