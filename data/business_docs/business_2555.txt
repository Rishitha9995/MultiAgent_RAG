Explain what a blockchain is in simple terms.
A blockchain is a type of database that is used to store data in a way that is secure, transparent, and decentralized. It is essentially a chain of blocks where each block contains a record of multiple transactions. These transactions are verified and validated by a network of users, rather than a central authority, making it a distributed database. Once the data is recorded in a block, it cannot be changed or deleted, creating an unalterable and permanent record. Blockchain technology is most commonly associated with cryptocurrencies, but it has a wide range of potential uses in fields such as supply chain management, voting systems, and identity verification.
