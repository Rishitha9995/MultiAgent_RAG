Brainstorm 4 possible options to reduce pollution in the city.
1. Implement public transportation: One of the most effective ways to reduce pollution in a city is to invest in an efficient public transportation network. This could include more buses and subways, and even expanding bike-sharing or carpooling programs. This can help to reduce the number of individual cars on the road, leading to a significant decrease in air pollution.

2. Promote green energy: Another option to reduce pollution in the city is to encourage the use of renewable energy sources, such as wind or solar power. This could be done by offering incentives to businesses and residents who switch to these cleaner forms of energy or by investing in city-wide green energy projects.

3. Increase green spaces: Adding more green spaces, such as parks, gardens, and green roofs, can also help to reduce pollution in the city. These spaces not only provide a place for residents to relax and enjoy nature, but also help to filter pollutants from the air and counteract the urban heat island effect.

4. Enforce environmental regulations: Strong and consistent enforcement of environmental regulations is another important step in reducing pollution within the city. This could include measures such as imposing fines for excessive emissions or enforcing stricter building codes to make new construction more environmentally friendly. By holding businesses and individuals accountable for their impact on the environment, cities can help to promote more sustainable practices.
