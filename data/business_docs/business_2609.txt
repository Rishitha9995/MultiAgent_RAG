Create a situation where a person might use the phrase "don't count your chickens before they hatch"
A person might use the phrase "don't count your chickens before they hatch" when they want to caution someone against making plans or assumptions that depend on a future outcome that is uncertain. For example, a friend tells you that they are going to buy a new car with the money they expect to earn from a business deal that hasn't been finalized yet. You might respond by saying "don't count your chickens before they hatch" to remind them that the deal might fall through and it's better to wait until the money is actually in hand before making plans to spend it.
