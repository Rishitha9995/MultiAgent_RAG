Suggest two design changes, for a generic company website, to improve the user experience.
Two design changes that could potentially improve the user experience of a generic company website:

1. Simplify the Navigation: One of the most important aspects of any website is its navigation, as it can make or break the user experience. A cluttered or complex navigation can make it difficult for users to find what they are looking for, leading to frustration and potentially causing them to leave the site. To improve the user experience, consider simplifying the navigation by reducing the number of menu items, grouping related pages together, and using clear and descriptive labels for each menu item.
2. Use More Visuals: Incorporating more visuals such as images, videos, and infographics can help to break up the text-heavy content on the website and make it more engaging and appealing to users. Visuals can also help to communicate complex ideas and information more effectively than text alone. However, it's important to ensure that the visuals used are high quality, relevant, and optimized for web viewing to avoid slowing down the website or negatively impacting the user experience.
