Analyze the following situation and explain why it could be seen as unethical.
A CEO’s decision-making process without consulting their board of directors may raise some serious ethical concerns. The board of directors serve as representatives of the shareholders' interest, and they have a fiduciary responsibility to protect the value of the company as well as ensure that it operates within legal and ethical bounds. A CEO is expected to work with the board in making decisions that are in the best interest of the company and its shareholders. When the CEO makes decisions unilaterally without consulting the board, it can be seen as an abuse of power and could potentially lead to decisions being made that may conflict with the company's values or harm the interests of the shareholders.

Such an action goes against the principles of transparency, accountability, and responsibility. It also undermines the role of the board, and it can create mistrust between the CEO, the board, and the shareholders. A healthy relationship between the board and CEO is necessary for effective corporate governance, and any actions that threaten this relationship could have a negative impact on the company. In summary, the CEO's decision to act independently without consulting the board could be seen as unethical because it goes against corporate governance principles, potentially harms the shareholders' interest, and undermines the trust and collaboration necessary for the effective functioning of the company.
