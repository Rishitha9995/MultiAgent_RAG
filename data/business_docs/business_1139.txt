Explain the concept of marginal revenue.
Marginal revenue (MR) is the additional revenue that a firm generates when it produces and sells one more unit of its product or service. In other words, it is the change in total revenue that comes about as a result of increasing output by one unit.

The concept of marginal revenue is important to businesses because it helps them understand the impact of increasing production on their bottom line. If a firm can accurately calculate its marginal revenue, it can use that information to determine the most profitable level of production.

Marginal revenue is closely related to the concept of price elasticity of demand. If demand for a product is elastic, meaning that consumers are sensitive to changes in price, a small increase in production may result in a large increase in revenue, since the increased quantity can be sold at a slightly lower price per unit. However, if demand is inelastic, meaning that consumers are relatively insensitive to changes in price, increasing production may not result in a significant increase in revenue, since the price per unit must be lowered by a larger amount to sell the additional units.
