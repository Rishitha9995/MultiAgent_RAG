Identify an example of a basic economic principle
One example of a basic economic principle is the law of supply and demand. This principle asserts that, all else being equal, an increase in the demand for a good or service will result in an increase in its price, while an increase in the supply of a good or service will result in a decrease in its price. In other words, the price of a good or service is determined by the relative levels of supply and demand. If demand exceeds supply, the price will rise; if supply exceeds demand, the price will fall. This principle is used to explain price movements in a wide range of markets, from consumer goods to financial assets.
