Suggest a creative solution to a rising problem in your school or community.
One rising problem in our communities today is food insecurity, with many people struggling to access affordable and nutritious food. A creative solution could be to introduce a "community food sharing" program that connects local farmers, businesses and residents to share and redistribute surplus or unused food.

Under this program, participating farmers and food businesses could donate their surplus produce or food items to a local distribution center. Volunteers from the community can then use this distribution center to organize and distribute the food to those in need, such as low-income families, elderly residents, or homeless individuals.

To help raise awareness and promote the program, schools can partner with other community stakeholders to organize food drives, awareness campaigns, and other events to encourage residents to get involved and support their neighbors in need.

By collaborating with local farmers, businesses, and volunteers, the community food sharing program not only helps to address the issue of food insecurity, but also promotes community cohesion, reduces food waste, and supports local agriculture.
