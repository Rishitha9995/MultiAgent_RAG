What does an algorithm do?
An algorithm is a sequence of step-by-step instructions or a set of rules used to solve a specific problem, perform a task or accomplish a particular goal. In computer science, algorithms are used in programming, where they form a basis for designing and writing computer programs that perform specific tasks such as searching, sorting or data analysis. They are also used in various other fields, including mathematics, engineering and finance, to solve problems and make decisions. An algorithm, by itself, does not perform any action; instead, it serves as a blueprint or guide for a computer or a human to carry out the defined process or task.
