Recap the storyline of the movie "The Godfather".
"The Godfather" is an American crime film released in 1972, directed by Francis Ford Coppola and based on the bestselling novel of the same name by Mario Puzo. The story takes place from 1945 to 1955 and follows the fictional Corleone family, a powerful and respected Italian-American Mafia clan operating in New York City.

The film opens with a wedding scene, as the daughter of the Godfather, Vito Corleone, is getting married. During the wedding, Vito is approached by a rival gangster, Sollozzo, who requests his support in the drug trade. Vito refuses, setting off a series of events that lead to attempts on his life.

After Vito is shot and nearly killed, his youngest son, Michael, steps up to take revenge against those who harmed his father. Michael proves to be calculating and ruthless, taking over the family business and becoming the new Godfather. He consolidates power and eliminates his rivals, solidifying the Corleone family’s status as a dominant force in the criminal underworld.

The film concludes with a montage of scenes showing the outcome of Michael's actions, as he lies to his wife about his criminal activity and becomes more and more engulfed in the world of the Mafia. The final shot shows the door closing as Michael is left alone, isolated in his new role as the Godfather.

"The Godfather" is widely regarded as one of the greatest films in world cinema and a landmark achievement in the gangster genre, revered for its complex characters, rich storytelling, and examination of the American Dream as seen through the eyes of a criminal dynasty.
