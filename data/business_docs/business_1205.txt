Identify a specific example of a conflict of interests and explain why it constitutes such a conflict.
An example of a conflict of interest could occur when a member of a city council is a local business owner in the same district where they serve. If there is a new zoning proposal being presented that would benefit their own business financially, the council member may not be able to make an unbiased decision that is in the best interest of their constituents. This is because they stand to gain personally from approving the proposal, and thus have a conflict of interest. In this case, the conflict arises between the council member’s duty to represent the interests of their community and their personal financial gain.
