Explain the stages of product development.
The process of bringing a new product into the market comprises various stages collectively referred to as product development. Below is an overview of the different stages involved.

1. Ideation: This is the first stage where ideas for new products are generated. It involves brainstorming and generating lots of possible ideas for a new product, before settling on one that aligns with the company's goals, values and target market.

2. Concept Development: This stage involves refining the idea and determining its feasibility. At this point, the company conducts preliminary market research to gauge consumer interest and demand for the product. They also create rough sketches or prototypes, and work with engineers or designers to determine the product's technical specifications and features.

3. Design & Development: Here the product design is refined and finalized, and the product is developed. This may involve creating detailed drawings, 3D models or prototypes. The company also works out the production process and develops a plan for manufacturing, testing and packaging the product.

4. Testing: Before the product hits the market, it must be rigorously tested to ensure it performs as expected and meets all relevant safety standards. This stage involves both internal testing by company engineers and external testing by independent organizations, as well as beta testing with a select group of customers.

5. Launch: Once the product has passed all tests and is ready for the market, it moves into the launch phase. This involves ramping up production, marketing the product heavily to generate demand, and distributing it through various channels to reach the target consumer. The launch phase is critical, and requires close attention to ensure that the product is positioned correctly and that the launch goes smoothly.

6. Post-Launch Evaluation: The final stage of product development is evaluation. This involves measuring the product's performance, gathering feedback from customers and analyzing sales data to determine how well the product is doing. The company may make changes to improve the product, or even discontinue it if it is not meeting expectations. The post-launch evaluation phase is crucial to the continued success of the product, and to the ongoing growth and development of the company.
