Follow the law of supply and demand, describe what would happen to the price of a good if the demand increased.
The law of supply and demand states that the price of a good is determined by the relationship between the supply of the good and the demand for that good. When demand for a good increases and the supply remains constant, the price of the good will increase. This is because there are more buyers competing for the same amount of the good, which creates a higher demand for the product. As a result, sellers can charge a higher price for the product because people are willing to pay more to obtain it. This continues until the market reaches a new equilibrium, where the supply of the good matches the new level of demand at the higher price.
