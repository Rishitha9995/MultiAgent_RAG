Name five types of financial markets in the United States.
1. Stock Market: It is a marketplace where shares of publicly held companies are issued and traded either through exchanges or over-the-counter markets.
2. Bond Market: It is a financial market where participants can issue new debt or buy and sell debt securities, usually in the form of bonds.
3. Commodity Market: It is a market that trades in primary economic sector rather than manufactured products, such as crude oil, gold and agricultural products.
4. Foreign Exchange Market: It is a global market for the trading of currencies. This market determines foreign exchange rates for every currency.
5. Derivatives Market: It is a financial market for derivatives, which are financial instruments that derive their value from an underlying asset. Common derivatives include futures contracts, options and swaps.
