List the five stages of the consumer decision making process.
The five stages of the consumer decision-making process are:

1. **Problem Recognition:** In this stage, the consumer identifies the problem, need, or want that they want to fulfill. This could be a result of internal stimuli, such as hunger or thirst, or external stimuli, such as an advertisement for a new product.

2. **Information Search:** Once the problem has been recognized, the consumer begins searching for information about the product or service that can fulfill their need or want. This can involve both internal and external sources of information.

3. **Evaluation of Alternatives:** In this stage, the consumer compares the different options available based on their own criteria, such as price, quality, features, or brand reputation.

4. **Purchase Decision:** Once the consumer has evaluated the alternatives, they make a decision about which product or service to purchase. At this stage, they may also consider other factors, such as sales promotions or the ease of the purchasing process.

5. **Post-Purchase Evaluation:** After the purchase has been made, the consumer assesses their decision to determine whether they are satisfied with their choice. If the product or service meets or exceeds their expectations, it is likely that they will become repeat customers or recommend the product to others. Alternatively, if the product doesn’t meet their expectations, the consumer may experience regret or disappointment. 

Each of these stages is an opportunity for marketers to communicate with the consumer and provide them with the information they need to make an informed decision.
