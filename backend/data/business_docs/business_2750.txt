Identify all the business risks associated with a company.
A company can be exposed to several business risks that may impact its ability to realize its objectives and achieve its goals. These risks vary depending on the industry, the size of the company, the specific operations, and many other factors. Some of the common business risks that a company may face include:

1. Market Risk: This is the risk of loss resulting from changes in market conditions such as interest rates, commodity prices, stock market fluctuations, and foreign exchange rates.

2. Credit Risk: This is the risk of loss resulting from a borrower or counterparty defaulting on a loan or other financial obligation.

3. Liquidity Risk: This is the risk that a company may not be able to meet its financial obligations or fund its operations due to an inability to convert assets into cash or access funding.

4. Operational Risk: This is the risk of loss resulting from inadequate or failed internal processes, systems, or human errors.

5. Compliance Risk: This is the risk of legal or regulatory sanctions, financial losses, or damage to reputation resulting from failure to comply with laws, regulations, and industry standards.

6. Strategic Risk: This is the risk of loss resulting from a failure to implement an appropriate business strategy or adapt to changes in the external environment.

7. Reputational Risk: This is the risk of loss resulting from damage to a company's reputation due to negative publicity, perception, or both.

8. Technology Risk: This is the risk of loss resulting from failures or disruptions in technology systems, cyber attacks, or other technology-related issues.

9. Country or Political Risk: This is the risk of loss resulting from political instability or changes in government policies in the countries where a company operates.

10. Environmental Risk: This is the risk of loss resulting from environmental issues such as climate change, natural disasters, or pollution.
