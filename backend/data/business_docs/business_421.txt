Describe the use of an insurance policy for a person.
An insurance policy is a contract between an individual and the insurance company that offers protection from financial loss in the event of an unexpected event. 

The policy provides the policyholder with coverage for specific risks, such as damage to property or illness, by paying out a sum of money in the event of a covered loss. 

The individual typically pays a premium or a regular payment to the insurance company for the coverage. In return, the insurance company promises to pay for any losses covered by the policy, up to the policy's limit, if and when such an incident occurs.

For a person, insurance can provide peace of mind and financial protection against unforeseen events, such as accidents, theft, or illness. For example, a health insurance policy can help cover medical expenses, while car insurance can mitigate the financial damages caused by an accident. 

In summary, an insurance policy for a person helps to minimize the financial risks of unexpected events, providing security and peace of mind.
