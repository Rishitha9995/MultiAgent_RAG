Create a fable featuring two animals by taking the following sentence as a beginning.
Once upon a time, a rabbit and a fox met in a forest. The rabbit was hopping happily along, gathering berries and nuts, when he happened upon the sly fox, who was out hunting for his next meal.

"Hello there, little rabbit," said the fox. "What are you doing all alone in this big, dangerous forest?"

"I'm not afraid," replied the rabbit. "I'm gathering food for the winter. I have plenty of wits to keep me safe."

The fox chuckled to himself. "Well, little rabbit, you may be clever, but you'll never be as sly and cunning as a fox." 

The rabbit simply smiled and continued on his way, minding his own business. But the fox was determined to show the rabbit how crafty he could be. So, he began to follow the rabbit, determined to catch him off guard.

The rabbit had a feeling that he was being followed, so he quickened his pace and began hopping and skipping around, dodging trees and bushes. The fox tried to keep up, but the little rabbit was just too fast.

Eventually, the fox grew tired and had to stop to catch his breath. And that's when the rabbit turned to him and said, "You see, Mr. Fox, it's not always about being sly and cunning. Sometimes, it's about being quick and clever."

The fox hung his head in shame, realizing that the little rabbit was right. He had underestimated the rabbit and let his arrogance get the best of him. From that day on, the fox and the rabbit became friends and they worked together to gather food for the winter, each using their unique abilities to help one another.

And the moral of the story is that everyone has their own unique strengths and talents, and it is important not to underestimate others or let our pride cloud our judgment.
