Explain what a "fiscal deficit" is.
A fiscal deficit refers to the situation where a government's spending exceeds its revenue within a given period, usually a fiscal year. In other words, it is the difference between the government's total expenditure and total receipts, excluding borrowings. To finance the deficit, the government may borrow from domestic or foreign sources, often by issuing securities such as treasury bonds, or through other means such as drawing on foreign exchange reserves. Large and persistent fiscal deficits may lead to an increase in public debt levels and may also have macroeconomic implications, such as higher interest rates and inflation. Therefore, managing the fiscal deficit is usually an important aspect of government's overall economic policy.
