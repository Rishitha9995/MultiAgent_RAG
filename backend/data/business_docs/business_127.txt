Explain the concept of exponential growth.
Exponential growth refers to a rate of increase that becomes increasingly rapid over time, resulting in an explosion in the growth of a quantity. This type of growth occurs when the rate of change of something is proportional to its current value. In other words, as the quantity grows, the rate of growth also grows, leading to a snowballing effect.

One popular example of exponential growth is population growth. If a population is increasing at a rate proportional to its size, then the larger the population gets, the faster it will continue to grow. Similarly, the concept of compounding interest is also an example of exponential growth, where the interest earned on an investment is added back to the principal, increasing the rate at which it grows.

Exponential growth is often represented graphically as a curve that starts off slowly, but then becomes steeper and steeper over time, and can be described mathematically using an exponential function. It's an important concept in many fields, including biology, finance, and physics, and is often used to model and predict the growth of various phenomena.

Overall, exponential growth represents a powerful force that can result in significant change over time. However, it's important to note that exponential growth can't continue indefinitely, since it will eventually reach physical or practical limitations.
