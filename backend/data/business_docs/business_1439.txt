Create a short story showcasing the friendship between two animals.
In the deep green forest, there lived a tiny mouse and a majestic elephant. They were the unlikeliest of friends, but their bond was stronger than any in the forest. Their friendship began when the quick-witted mouse outsmarted the hunter's trap, releasing the elephant who had been caught. From that day on, the elephant promised the mouse that he would always protect him.

The two friends were inseparable, spending their days playing by the river, chasing each other in the meadow, sharing sweet fruits, and telling each other stories under the stars. The elephant would use his trunk to lift the mouse onto his back, and they'd go on adventures, exploring every inch of the forest. The mouse, in return, would tell the elephant all the best spots to find the juiciest leaves and the sweetest berries.

The animals of the forest often wondered how the two could be such good friends, given their vast differences in size, speed, and strength. But the mouse and the elephant knew that true friendship knows no bounds, and they were grateful for each other's company.

Although their friendship was unusual, it was pure and true. They taught the animals of the forest that it doesn't matter how different two friends may seem, what matters is the love that they share. The mouse and the elephant's friendship was a shining example of what it means to be a true friend, and their bond endured through all the seasons of their lives.
