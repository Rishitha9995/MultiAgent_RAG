Describe a risk management framework.
A risk management framework is a structured approach designed to identify, assess, prioritize and mitigate risks that a business or organization may face. The process involves several steps that can help lower the probability of negative events and reduce their potential impact. Here are the key components of a risk management framework:

1. Risk Identification: The first step in risk management involves identifying potential threats or risks that can have an adverse impact on the organization. Risks can range from financial, legal, operational, strategic, to reputational, among others.

2. Risk Assessment: Once the risks have been identified, the next step involves evaluating the probability of occurrence and the potential impact each risk may have. This information will help determine the severity of the risk and the level of attention required to address it.

3. Risk Prioritization: Based on the assessment, risks are prioritized in terms of their potential impact and likelihood of occurrence. This helps the organization allocate resources and focus on managing the most significant risks.

4. Risk Mitigation: This involves developing and implementing action plans to mitigate the risks. These plans could include risk prevention, risk transfer, or risk reduction strategies, among others.

5. Monitoring and Review: Risk management is an ongoing process that requires regular monitoring and review. This involves tracking the progress of the risk mitigation plans, assessing their effectiveness, and making changes as necessary.

In conclusion, a risk management framework provides a structured approach for managing risks and helps ensure that organizations are better prepared to deal with uncertainty and potential negative events.
