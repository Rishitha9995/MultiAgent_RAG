Some of the issues with remote work that have arisen for employers due to the global pandemic include communication and collaboration challenges, as working remotely means teams are no longer physically located in the same space. This can make it more difficult to have spontaneous and informal interactions and can also make it harder to build and maintain team cohesion. Other issues include reduced oversight and supervision, as employers may find it more difficult to monitor employees' productivity and work progress. This can also create challenges for maintaining company culture and worker morale. Additionally, there are concerns around data security and confidentiality as employees work from home on potentially less secure networks. Finally, remote work can also present challenges with work-life balance, as the boundaries between personal and professional life become more blurred, which can lead to job-related stress and burnout.
