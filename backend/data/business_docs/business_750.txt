Create an original analogy to explain the concept of 'data abstraction'.
Suppose you plan to cook a complex meal for dinner. You look up a recipe to follow. The recipe offers the list of ingredients, the measurements, and the step by step procedures to prepare the meal. Now, imagine if instead of giving you the list of ingredients and their measurements, it asks you to go to the farm or supermarket to pick out each grain, vegetable, and condiment one by one - this would make the process extremely tedious and overwhelming. This is similar to how data abstraction works in computer science. Just like the recipe summarizes the ingredients for you, data abstraction simplifies and summarizes larger and more complex chunks of data for a programmer, preventing them from being overwhelmed or lost in the complexity of the data itself.
