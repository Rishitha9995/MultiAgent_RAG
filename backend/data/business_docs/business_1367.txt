Tell me about the traditional Philippine clothing for men.
The traditional clothing for men in the Philippines is known as the "Barong Tagalog" or simply "Barong." It is a formal long-sleeved, usually made from lightweight and transparent fabrics such as piña, jusi or organza. The barong is embroidered with intricate designs, often featuring geometric patterns or natural elements such as leaves and flowers.

The barong is typically worn over an undershirt, and can be paired with black dress slacks or traditional Filipino trousers known as "saya" or "salawal." It is usually left untucked, and can be fastened with buttons or left open at the front.

The Barong is usually worn during special occasions such as weddings, ceremonies, and formal events, although it may also be worn as business attire. It is considered as a symbol of Filipino heritage and national identity, and is a source of pride for many Filipinos.
