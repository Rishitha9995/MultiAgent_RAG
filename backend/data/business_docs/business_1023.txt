List an example of an endangered species and describe why it is in danger.
One example of an endangered species is the Amur leopard. The Amur leopard, also known as the Far Eastern leopard or the Korean leopard, is a subspecies of leopard native to the southeastern region of Russia and northeastern China. This species has been classified as critically endangered by the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) since 1996.

One of the main reasons why the Amur leopard is in danger is because of habitat loss. The rapid expansion of human populations in the region, as well as the development of infrastructure, has led to the destruction of much of the leopard's natural habitat. The conversion of forests into farmland or other forms of land use has severely reduced the number of prey species available for the leopards to hunt, leaving them with limited options for food.

Poaching is another significant factor contributing to the decline of the Amur leopard population. Despite protective laws prohibiting the hunting and killing of Amur leopards, poaching remains a problem in the region. Amur leopards are targeted for their beautiful fur, which is sold illegally on the black market.

Inbreeding and disease also pose a threat to the survival of the Amur leopard. With such a small population size, it is difficult to maintain genetic diversity, which can result in inbreeding and increased susceptibility to diseases. 

Conservation efforts are underway to help protect and recover the Amur leopard population. These efforts include habitat restoration, increased protection against poaching, and captive breeding programs.
