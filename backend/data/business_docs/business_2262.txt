Describe what a customer service representative should do.
A customer service representative should engage with customers in a professional and friendly manner, actively listening to their concerns, questions or feedback, and solve problems by providing accurate information or offering solutions. Duties may vary depending on the industry and specific role, but common tasks include:

1. Answering customer inquiries: A representative is responsible for answering customers' questions about products or services, account information, or order status. This can be in person, over the phone, via chat or email.

2. Resolving customer complaints: If a customer has a problem, the representative should handle the issue as soon as possible, taking the appropriate action to resolve the situation.

3. Providing customer support: A representative may assist customers with making purchases, setting up accounts, or troubleshooting technical issues.

4. Handling returns or exchanges: If a customer wants to return or exchange a product, the representative should handle this process, following company policies and procedures.

5. Documenting interactions: A representative should keep detailed records of customer interactions and transactions, including any complaints or issues that arose.

6. Collaborating with team members: In some cases, a representative may need to work with other team members or departments to resolve a customer’s issue.

Overall, a customer service representative should strive to provide excellent service, making the customer feel valued and supported. This includes being empathetic, courteous, and professional at all times.
