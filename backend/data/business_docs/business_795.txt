Generate a list of 10 ideas for activities to do at an outreach event to promote interest in psychology.
1. A social experiment that gets people to participate and interact, demonstrating principles of group psychology.
2. A fun quiz or game that incorporates psychological concepts and facts to engage and teach participants.
3. Hosting a talk or a workshop on stress management and self-care with tips and techniques based on psychological research.
4. Setting up an interactive exhibit on optical illusions or other perceptual phenomena to spark curiosity and interest in perception and cognitive psychology.
5. Prizes for participating in a blindfolded sensory experience, aimed at showing how our other senses become more acute when one is taken away.
6. Showing short videos or documentaries on famous psychological experiments and their impact on the field.
7. A 'meet the expert' session, where participants can interact with a working psychologist and learn more about the profession and their day-to-day work.
8. An art therapy booth, where people can engage in a creative art activity while learning about the benefits of art therapy for mental health.
9. A memory challenge that demonstrates the fallibility of our memories by showing how easily they can be manipulated or distorted.
