What constitutes a true friendship?
True friendship is based on mutual trust, respect, and support. It involves deep connections and genuine affection between two people who value each other's company and are willing to share their thoughts, feelings, and experiences. A true friend is someone who is always there for you, through good times and bad, offering a listening ear and a shoulder to lean on when you need it. They are honest and loyal, and they are willing to offer constructive criticism or advice when necessary. True friendship goes beyond mere acquaintance or casual interactions and is a bond that lasts over time, built through shared experiences, meaningful conversations, and mutual understanding.
