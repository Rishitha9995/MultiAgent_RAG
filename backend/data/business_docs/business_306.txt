Demonstrate the definition of the term "law of supply and demand".
The law of supply and demand is an economic theory that explains the interaction between buyers and sellers in the market, determining the price and quantity of goods and services exchanged. In simple terms, the law of supply and demand states that when the demand for a good or service increases, the price also increases, and when the demand decreases, the price also decreases. Conversely, when the supply of a good or service increases, the price tends to decrease, and when the supply decreases, the price tends to increase. This relationship between supply, demand, and price is fundamental to understanding how markets work, and it helps to explain the allocation of resources in an economy.
