Write a biography of Anna Pavlova, a famous prima ballerina.
Anna Pavlova was a Russian prima ballerina, widely considered to be one of the most famous and influential dancers of her time. Born on February 12, 1881, in St. Petersburg, Russia, she was the daughter of a laundress, and her father's identity remains unclear. Pavlova’s passion for ballet began at a young age when her mother took her to see a performance of Tchaikovsky's The Sleeping Beauty at the Imperial Maryinsky Theatre.

Pavlova was accepted into the Imperial Ballet School at the age of ten, where she trained under the tutelage of Enrico Cecchetti, Christian Johansson, and Pavel Gerdt. Though her physique was not considered ideal for ballet, her determination and artistry earned her a place in the Imperial Ballet. She made her debut in 1899 at the age of 18, and her skill and grace quickly earned her the title of prima ballerina.

In 1907, Pavlova created her iconic role, The Dying Swan, which was choreographed for her by Michel Fokine. The solo dance, set to the music of Camille Saint-Saens, is a portrayal of a swan's last moments as it gracefully succumbs to death. Pavlova's interpretation of the role was so powerful that it became synonymous with her name.

Pavlova left the Imperial Ballet in 1913 to form her own dance company, with which she toured extensively throughout the world. She introduced ballet to many countries where it was previously unknown, and her performances were met with great enthusiasm by audiences everywhere. Pavlova's grace, technique, and emotional depth helped to establish the art of ballet as a respected and popular form of entertainment.

Anna Pavlova continued to dance and tour until her death in 1931, at the age of 49, from pleurisy. Though her life was cut short, her legacy lives on through the countless dancers she inspired, and through the enduring popularity of her most famous role, The Dying Swan. Pavlova was a true artist, whose passion and dedication to her craft left an indelible mark on the world of dance.
