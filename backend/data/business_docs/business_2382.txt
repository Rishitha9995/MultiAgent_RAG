Determine the most suitable context to use the phrase "on the cards".
The phrase "on the cards" means that something is very likely to happen or is planned to occur. It is commonly used when discussing potential events or future outcomes in various contexts such as personal life and career, business, and politics. 

Examples of contexts where the phrase "on the cards" could be used appropriately may include:

- In a conversation between friends discussing the possibility of going on a trip together: "Hey, do you think a road trip to the mountains is on the cards for next month?"
- During a business meeting while forecasting future market trends: "Our analysts predict that an increase in demand for our products is on the cards in the third quarter."
- In a sports news report discussing a player's future movements: "Rumors have been circulating that a transfer to a new team may be on the cards for the star player."
- During political analysis, when discussing potential outcomes: "Early polls suggest that a change in government is on the cards in the upcoming election."
