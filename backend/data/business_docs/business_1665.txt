What are the differences between a growth hacker and an entrepreneur?
A growth hacker and an entrepreneur are two distinct roles that have some key differences:

1. Goals and objectives: An entrepreneur seeks to bring new ideas or business ventures to life, taking on the responsibility for managing them from the ground up. A growth hacker’s main goal is to find new strategies, techniques and tools to generate massive and rapid growth in their user base or customer acquisition, with a keen focus on data and user behavior.

2. Approach and mindset: An entrepreneur has a broad focus that encompasses all aspects of starting and running a business. A growth hacker, on the other hand, has a more specific focus on growth alone, and often experiments with several different approaches at the same time to find the most effective way to drive growth.

3. Scope of responsibilities: Entrepreneurs have a wide range of responsibilities, from managing finances, people, and business operations, to creating and executing a business plan. Growth hackers deal mainly with marketing, analytics and product development, with the aim of optimizing growth.

In summary, while an entrepreneur takes a broader view of starting and running a business, a growth hacker has a more narrow focus on driving growth. That said, both share a similar drive for innovation and experimentation, and the two roles often work hand-in-hand in an entrepreneurial environment.
