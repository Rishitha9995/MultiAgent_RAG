Write a short story with an unexpected twist.
Once upon a time, there was a baby girl who was born into a poor family. Her parents were farmers who worked hard to provide for their family, but they never seemed to have enough. Despite their difficulties, they loved their daughter dearly and cherished every moment with her. As she grew older, the little girl became aware of her family's poverty, and she wished with all her heart that she could do something to help.

One day, a fairy appeared before the little girl and granted her a wish. Without hesitation, the girl wished for a better life for her parents. The fairy smiled and vanished, leaving the girl wondering if her wish would ever come true.

The next day, the girl's father went to the market to sell their crops, and to his amazement, he received more money than he had ever earned before. The family was overjoyed, and they thanked their lucky stars for their sudden change in fortune.

This stroke of good luck continued for many years, and the family's troubles were a thing of the past. The little girl grew up and got married, and her parents lived a long and happy life.

As the girl sat by her parents' deathbed many years later, she couldn't help but feel grateful for the fairy who had granted her wish. And at that moment, the fairy appeared once more.

"Your wish was granted," said the fairy, "but it was not because of my magic. You see, the love you have for your parents is the most powerful magic in the world, and it was that love that brought you all the happiness you deserved."

And with that, the fairy vanished once again, leaving the girl with a newfound appreciation for the power of love.

The twist in this short story is that the fairy reveals that it was not her magic that granted the girl's wish, but rather the love the girl had for her parents.
