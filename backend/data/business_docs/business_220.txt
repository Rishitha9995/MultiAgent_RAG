Design a system to mitigate the effects of climate change.
The complexities of climate change and its far-reaching consequences necessitate a multi-faceted and dynamic approach. However, a possible system to mitigate the effects of climate change could be built upon these key steps:

1. **Reducing carbon footprint:** Fossil fuels are the largest source of human-caused greenhouse gas emissions, primarily carbon dioxide. Industries, governments, and individuals can contribute to reducing their carbon footprint by switching to low-carbon and renewable energy, investing in energy-efficient technology, and changing our behavior patterns - like reducing consumption, recycling, and using public transportation or low emission vehicles.

2. **Carbon Sequestration:** A critical way to reduce carbon dioxide levels is through carbon sequestration techniques, such as afforestation, reforestation, soil carbon sequestration, ocean fertilization, and bioenergy with carbon capture and storage.

3. **Adaptation to Climate Change:** As we work towards reducing emissions, we also have to adapt to the changing climate. This includes building infrastructure and preparing for extreme weather events; increasing the resilience of our agriculture and water management systems; investing in early warning systems and risk assessment; and preparing vulnerable communities for the impacts of climate change.

4. **Encouraging sustainable practices:** Governments, corporations, and individuals all have a role to play in promoting sustainable practices - reducing, reusing, and recycling. This could be achieved through policy changes and incentives, such as subsidies, taxes, regulations, or CSR initiatives. Additionally, the proliferation of eco-friendly products, green buildings, and sustainable transportation options can make a significant difference.

5. **International Collaboration:** Climate change is a global problem that requires global cooperation. Because this issue crosses borders, it necessitates international agreements, frameworks, and organizations that enable countries and corporations to work together towards common goals. Further investment in climate research, climate financing, and technology transfer is also necessary.

In conclusion, a system to mitigate climate change would be designed to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, promote sustainable practices, foster adaptation, and encourage international collaboration.
