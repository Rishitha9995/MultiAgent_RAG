Indicate how the following characters in the novel reflect the theme of "the corrupting influence of power".
In Émile Zola’s novel Germinal, Gregoire is one of the characters who reflects the theme of the corrupting influence of power. Being a member of the bourgeoisie, Gregoire is portrayed as an owner of shares in the mining company that represents an upper class of the society that exploits the poor working class. He plays a key role in the story in the way he uses his influential power to maintain the capitalist social order.

Throughout the novel, Gregoire displays an attitude of complacency and acceptance of the social status quo, showing little concern or empathy towards the plight of the working-class miners. In fact, he presents himself as viewing the miners simply as part of the machinery of his business rather than real human beings, and he refuses to acknowledge their suffering or the injustices they endure. Instead, Gregoire justifies the company’s actions towards the workers, citing the need for profits and stability in the industry.

Gregoire’s behavior can be seen as a reflection of the corrupting nature of power. His position of influence, wealth, and prestige has clouded his judgment and morality, causing him to disregard the basic human needs and rights of the miners. In the end, his desire to maintain his position and status perpetuates the cycle of exploitation and oppression of the working class.
