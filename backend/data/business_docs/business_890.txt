Make a list of 3 possible causes of the blackouts.
1. Electrical failures: One of the most common causes of blackouts is the failure of the electrical grid. This could be due to a variety of reasons such as equipment malfunction, natural disasters, or human error. Power stations, transformers, and transmission lines all play a crucial role in delivering electricity to homes and businesses, and a problem with any of these components could result in a power outage.

2. Overload of the power grid: Blackouts can also occur when the demand for electricity exceeds the capacity of the power grid. This is particularly common during heatwaves or cold spells when people heavily rely on air conditioners or heaters, as well as during special events that draw large crowds. When energy consumption reaches its peak, some areas may experience rolling blackouts, which are controlled power outages that are intended to prevent a major blackout.

3. Natural disasters: Earthquakes, hurricanes, tornadoes, thunderstorms, and other natural disasters can cause widespread power outages by damaging power plants, transmission lines, or other infrastructure. In some cases, utility companies may intentionally shut off power to certain areas to prevent fires or other hazards, particularly in windy conditions when live power lines may come into contact with trees or other flammable materials.
