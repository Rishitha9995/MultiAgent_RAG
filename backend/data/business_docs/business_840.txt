What are the economic impacts of the global pandemic?
The global pandemic has had a far-reaching impact on the world economy. The immediate effect was seen in the form of massive job losses as businesses closed down in numerous countries. The International Labor Organization (ILO) has estimated that 25 million jobs could be lost worldwide due to the pandemic. Many businesses have also reported a decline in revenue, leading to wage cuts and further layoffs.

The pandemic has hit global trade, with many countries shutting their borders, and there are disruptions in the supply chain. International tourism has also been affected as most countries have closed their borders to tourists, leading to a huge impact on the income of countries that depend on tourism.

On the macroeconomic level, the pandemic has led to a decline in the global GDP. According to the International Monetary Fund (IMF), the world economy is expected to shrink by 3% in 2020, marking the worst recession since the 1930s. Governments around the globe have had to introduce stimulus packages and financial measures to support their economies, which has led to an increase in public debt.

In summary, the economic impacts of the global pandemic are huge, leading to job losses, a decline in GDP, disruptions in global trade, and an increase in public debt. Governments around the globe are actively working to mitigate these impacts and support their economies during this challenging time.
