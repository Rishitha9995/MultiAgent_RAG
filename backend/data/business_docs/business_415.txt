Here is a potential solution for communication issues within a team: 

1. Identify the root cause: Start by identifying what is causing the communication issues. This could involve talking to team members to get their perspective or observing team interactions to identify where problems are arising.

2. Set clear communication guidelines: Define the channels of communication that should be used for different types of information. For instance, establish which types of messages should be sent via email and which ones should be discussed in meetings. 

3. Hold regular team meetings: Schedule regular team meetings to ensure that everyone is on the same page. Use these meetings to discuss progress, set goals, and address any issues that have arisen.

4. Encourage open communication: Create an environment where team members feel comfortable sharing their thoughts and ideas. Address any conflicts promptly to prevent them from escalating.

5. Utilize technology: Utilize technology such as team messaging apps or project management tools to streamline communication and keep everyone in the loop on important updates.

6. Monitor progress: Regularly check in on the team to ensure that communication is improving. If issues persist, reassess the situation and adjust the approach as needed.

By implementing these steps, the team can improve communication, leading to better collaboration and productivity.
