Explain why the corporate tax rate was lowered to 17%
The corporate tax rate was lowered to 17% as a part of a larger tax reform effort by the government. There are several reasons why the government may have chosen to do this:

1. Increased competitiveness: A lower corporate tax rate can make a country a more attractive location for businesses, potentially bringing in more investment and boosting the economy.

2. Encourage growth and job creation: Lower corporate taxes can encourage businesses to invest more, expand operations, and create new jobs, which can contribute to the overall growth of the economy.

3. Ease the tax burden on businesses: Reducing the corporate tax rate can help to ease the financial burden on businesses, which may free up capital they can use to invest in their operations, increase wages, or pay higher dividends to shareholders.

4. Simplification of the tax code: Lowering the corporate tax rate can be part of a broader effort to simplify the tax code, making it easier for businesses to understand and comply with their tax obligations.

It is important to note, however, that the specific reasons behind lowering the corporate tax rate to 17 % may vary by country, so it is always a good idea to consult local governmental publications for comprehensive reasons on this topic.
