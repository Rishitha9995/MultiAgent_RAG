What are the four attributes of an effective mission statement?
An effective mission statement is typically characterized by four main attributes: clarity, concision, inspiration, and purpose.

1. Clarity: An effective mission statement is clear and easy to understand. It should be written in simple language and avoid technical jargon or complex concepts. It should be straightforward and leave no room for ambiguity.

2. Concision: An effective mission statement is concise and to the point. It should capture the essence of the company's values and purpose in just a few words. A well-crafted mission statement is brief and memorable.

3. Inspiration: An effective mission statement should inspire and motivate employees and stakeholders. It should be aspirational and paint a picture of what the company stands for, and what it hopes to achieve.

4. Purpose: Above all, an effective mission statement should clearly communicate the company's purpose. It should explain why the company exists and what it aims to accomplish. The mission statement should serve as a guide for decision making and provide direction for the organization.
