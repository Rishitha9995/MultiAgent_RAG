Explain what the stock market is
The stock market refers to the collection of markets and exchanges where the issuing and trading of stocks or shares of publicly-held companies, bonds, and other securities takes place. These financial instruments are bought and sold by investors, traders and other market participants. Companies issue their stocks in order to raise capital to fund their business operations, while investors and traders buy and sell these stocks with the aim of generating profits. The prices of stocks in the market fluctuate, based on various factors such as the company's financial performance, market trends, and the overall economic climate. Stock markets play a crucial role in the global economy, enabling capital to flow between savers and borrowers, and helping companies to grow and develop.
