Summarize the current state of renewable energy in the US.
As of 2021, the renewable energy industry in the US is growing rapidly, and renewable sources of energy like wind and solar have become increasingly cost-competitive with traditional fossil fuel sources. In 2020, renewable energy accounted for about 12% of total energy consumption and about 20% of electricity generation. The growth in renewable energy has been driven by a combination of policy and market factors, including federal tax incentives, state renewable portfolio standards, and the declining cost of renewable technologies. The most dominant sources of renewable energy in the US are wind, solar, and hydropower, while bioenergy and geothermal energy have a smaller and more localized presence. The Biden administration has also set ambitious goals to transition the US to 100% clean energy by 2035, and has proposed significant investments in renewable energy infrastructure and research. Overall, the future of renewable energy in the US looks promising, with continued growth expected in the coming years.
