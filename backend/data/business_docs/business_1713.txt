Explain what a free market economy is.
A free market economy is an economic system where supply and demand are the primary drivers of prices and production, and where private individuals and businesses play the major role in making economic decisions. In a free market, money transactions between buyers and sellers occur freely, in the absence of external influence or coercion from the government, monopolies or cartels. Prices for goods and services are set freely by the forces of supply and demand and are allowed to reach their point of equilibrium without government intervention. Government's role in a free market system is to protect property rights, promote competition, regulate industries to protect consumers and ensure a level playing field for businesses. A free market economy is characterized by the ability of individuals to innovate, create businesses and invest in new ideas, leading to economic growth, increased competition and greater choice for consumers.
