Describe the impact of industrialization on a country’s economy.
Industrialization has a significant impact on a country's economy, driving growth and development through increased productivity, efficiency and technological advancements. This results in the shift from an agrarian-based economy to one that is dominated by manufacturing and industry.

One of the major effects of industrialization is the increase in employment opportunities, as new factories, businesses and industries are established to meet growing demand for goods and services. This creates jobs, improves standards of living and reduces poverty. Additionally, industrialization increases the demand for raw materials, which in turn stimulates the growth of primary industries such as mining, agriculture, and forestry.

As the economy grows, infrastructure improves and there is greater investment in technological research and development, leading to innovations that increase efficiency and productivity. This results in the growth of complementary industries, such as transportation and communication, which facilitates the expansion of businesses and markets.

Industrialization also drives intensification of trade and commerce, both domestically and internationally. A country with industrialized economies often exports its goods and services to other countries, leading to increased foreign exchange earnings, a stronger currency and better international trading relations.

Overall, the impact of industrialization on a country’s economy is significant as it drives economic growth through increased productivity, job creation, improved infrastructure, technological advancements, and expanded markets. However, it is important to note that industrialization, if not managed properly, can also have negative environmental and social impacts. Therefore, there must be a balance between economic growth and sustainable development.
