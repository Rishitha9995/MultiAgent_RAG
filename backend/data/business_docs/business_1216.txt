Please explain what is anomaly detection and its applications.
Anomaly detection refers to the process of identifying data points, events, or observations that deviate significantly from the expected norm or pattern in a dataset. These deviations, often referred to as outliers, can be indicative of a fault or flaw in the system under observation, malicious activities, or even previously unknown trends or relationships.

Anomaly detection has applications in various fields such as finance, healthcare, cybersecurity, retail, and manufacturing, to name a few. In finance, anomaly detection techniques can be used to detect fraudulent activities such as abnormal credit card transactions. In healthcare, anomaly detection can help in identifying uncharacteristic changes in a patient’s medical data, like heart rate or blood pressure, which may be indicative of a worsening condition. In cybersecurity, anomaly detection can help in detecting intrusions or unauthorized accesses to a system. In a retail setting, anomaly detection can help in identifying sudden dips or spikes in sales, which may be indicative of a bigger issue, such as a supply chain problem. In the manufacturing industry, anomaly detection can be used to identify potential faults in machinery or equipment, by monitoring their performance data and detecting sudden changes or deviations from normal behavior.

Overall, anomaly detection helps in identifying previously unknown issues, trends, or relationships, by analyzing large amounts of data, and is an important tool in various fields and industries.
