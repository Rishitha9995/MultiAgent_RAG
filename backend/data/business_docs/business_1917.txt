Tell me an example of something that would cause a financial crisis.
One example of something that could cause a financial crisis would be a major economic shock, such as a housing market bubble bursting, causing widespread impacts on financial institutions, markets and the broader economy. Other factors that could contribute to a financial crisis might include rapidly rising debts, high levels of inflation, or a sudden change in investor confidence. Ultimately, a financial crisis can arise when there is significant instability and uncertainty within the financial system, often fed by a combination of global economic factors and domestic policy challenges.
