Solve this in python:

Edward is an Alchemist. In order to make money, he sells gold and silver on the market. However, gold is rare, so sometimes Edward uses alchemy to make lead look like gold.
He must be careful! If he tries to sell too much fake gold, people will find out. The quantity of fake gold must be strictly less than half of the total (fake + real) gold sold.

The value of the three metals are:
Pb - 1 coin
Ag - 10 coins
Au - 100 coins

Input:
A string metals with the name of the metals followed by the quantity, an integer, separated by a space
There are always three metals Silver Ag, Lead Pb, and Gold Au, in this order

e.g. Ag 27 Pb 10 Au 5

Output:
You must output the maximum value Edward can make by selling metals.
Here is one way to solve the problem in Python:

```
metals = input("Enter the quantities of metals (in the format 'Ag n Pb m Au p'): ").split()

# Convert the quantities to integers
ag_qty = int(metals[1])
pb_qty = int(metals[3])
au_qty = int(metals[5])

# Calculate the maximum quantity of fake gold that can be sold
max_fake_gold = (au_qty + pb_qty) // 2 - au_qty

# Make sure the maximum quantity is non-negative
max_fake_gold = max(0, max_fake_gold)

# Calculate the total value of the metals Edward can sell
total_value = ag_qty * 10 + pb_qty + (au_qty + max_fake_gold) * 100

print("Maximum value: {} coins".format(total_value))
```
