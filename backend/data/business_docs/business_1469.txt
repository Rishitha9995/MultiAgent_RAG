Generate a persuasive argument for why schools should require students to learn coding.
In today's world, technology is increasingly becoming a fundamental part of our daily lives. It has transformed the way we learn, work, and interact with each other. As the world continues to advance technologically, it's essential that students learn coding as a fundamental skill to prepare them for the future. Here are a few reasons why schools should require students to learn coding:

1. Coding is a valuable skill in the job market: With the ever-growing demand for technology, the job market for computer science and coding is rapidly expanding. It's predicted that there will be 1.4 million computing jobs available by 2020, and only 400,000 students qualified to fill them. By learning to code, students are opening up a world of opportunities for themselves in the future job market.

2. Coding promotes critical thinking and problem-solving: Coding requires a great deal of logical thinking and problem-solving. As students learn to code, they are also learning how to approach problems in a structured and methodical way. This skill is not only applicable in computer science, but in all areas of life.

3. Coding encourages creativity and innovation: When students learn to code, they learn how to create something from nothing. They learn to take an idea and turn it into something tangible. This fosters a sense of creativity and encourages students to think outside of the box and be innovative.

4. Coding prepares students for the future: As technology continues to advance, it's essential that students are equipped with the skills to navigate the digital world. By learning to code, students are preparing themselves for a future where technology is a fundamental part of our daily lives.

In conclusion, schools should require students to learn coding as it is a valuable skill in the job market, promotes critical thinking and problem-solving, encourages creativity and innovation, and prepares students for the future. By equipping students with this fundamental skill, we are setting them up for success in the digital world.
