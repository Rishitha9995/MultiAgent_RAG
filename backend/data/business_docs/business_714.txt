Design an experiment to test the effectiveness of a new type of tires.
Here is one way to design an experiment to test the effectiveness of a new type of tires:

1. **Determine the performance measures:** First, we should decide on which performance measures we would like to evaluate the new tires on. For instance, these measures can include grip, wear and tear, handling, fuel efficiency or speed.

2. **Select a control group:** We need to select a control group of vehicles equipped with standard tires that are typically seen on the market. These vehicles should be similar to those that will be equipped with the new tires to ensure the results are comparable.

3. **Equip the test group:** Next, we will equip a separate group of vehicles with the new type of tires. These vehicles should also be similar to the control group in terms of make, model, and year.

4. **Design the test course:** We must design a test course that would allow us to evaluate the performance measures chosen in step 1. For instance, the course may include straightaways and curves to test handling and speed, rough terrain for tire wear, and wet pavement to test grip.

5. **Run the experiment:** We'll run the control and test groups through the test course and collect data on the performance measures. We should make sure that the test conditions are similar for both groups to ensure fairness in the results.

6. **Analyze the results:** After collecting the data, we will analyze it to compare the performance of the new tires against the standard tires in the control group. We can use statistical analysis to determine whether any differences observed are significant.

7. **Draw conclusions:** Based on the results, we'll draw conclusions on the effectiveness of the new type of tires. If the new tires outperform the standard tires in the control group, then it suggests that they are more effective. On the other hand, if there is no significant difference between the two, it means that the new tires are not necessarily more effective than standard tires.
