Please explain the phrase "to play the long game".
"To play the long game" is an expression that refers to a strategy that involves planning and taking actions that may not yield immediate results, but instead focuses on achieving long-term goals and objectives. Instead of seeking instant gratification, a person who is playing the long game is willing to exercise patience, invest time and effort, and make sacrifices in the present in order to secure greater rewards in the future. This approach requires discipline, foresight, and a strong vision for what a person wants to achieve down the road. The expression is used both in the context of personal goals as well as in business, politics, and other fields where long-term planning and strategizing is important.
