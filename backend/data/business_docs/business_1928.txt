Define a stochastic process.
A stochastic process is a mathematical model for a sequence of events or values that evolve over time in a probabilistic manner. It is often used to represent systems or phenomena that are subject to randomness or uncertainty. Each event or value in the sequence is described by a random variable, and the probabilities associated with the values of these random variables are determined by a probability distribution. The main characteristic of a stochastic process is its dependence on time, where the probabilities can change as time evolves. There are many different types of stochastic processes, such as Markov chains, Poisson processes, and random walks, each with its own set of assumptions and properties. Stochastic processes are widely used in fields such as finance, economics, physics, and engineering to model and predict the behavior of complex systems.
