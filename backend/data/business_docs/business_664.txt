Given the historical event, hypothesize the potential economic impact it could have had, and factors you considered.
The closed border policy during the Ming Dynasty likely led to economic stagnation in China. As trade was restricted, this would have negatively affected the growth of the Chinese economy. Fewer exports of valuable goods meant lower profits for businesses, which in turn could have led to less innovation due to less incentive for entrepreneurs. Factors considered include the reduction of cross-cultural exchange, the potential decline of industries relying on export, and lower exposure to new goods and ideas from abroad.
