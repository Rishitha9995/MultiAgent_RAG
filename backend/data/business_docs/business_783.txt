Offer an example of when an algorithm was wrong
One well-known example of an algorithm getting it wrong was in the case of the "Flash Crash" of May 6, 2010. This event saw the Dow Jones Industrial Average plummet by almost 1000 points in a matter of minutes, before recovering almost as quickly. The cause of this sudden drop was found to be rogue algorithms that were designed to trade shares at high speeds. These algorithms were triggered by a large sell order for a particular stock, which caused them to enter a feedback loop of selling and buying, leading to the dramatic plunge in the stock market. This event demonstrated how even the most sophisticated algorithms can be wrong and cause significant consequences.
