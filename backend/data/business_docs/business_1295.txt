What impact does climate change have on humans?
Climate change has a significant impact on the lives and wellbeing of humans. Some of the ways in which it affects us include:

1. Health: Extreme weather events, such as heatwaves, floods, and droughts, can result in an increased occurrence of respiratory and cardiovascular diseases, injuries, malnutrition, and mental health problems.

2. Food security: Changes in temperature and precipitation patterns, together with an increase in extreme weather events, can reduce crop yields and affect food quality, leading to food shortages and higher prices.

3. Water availability: Climate change can alter the distribution of rainfall, snowmelt, river flows, and groundwater, resulting in water shortages in some regions and floods in others.

4. Economic impacts: Climate change can cause damages to infrastructure, disrupt business operations, and reduce productivity, leading to significant economic losses.

5. Forced migration and conflict: As climate change exacerbates resource scarcity and undermines livelihoods, it can lead to displacement, forced migration and increase the risk of conflict over scarce resources.

Overall, climate change is a global challenge that demands urgent and collective action to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, and to adapt to its already-unavoidable impacts.
