Create a timeline of computer technology from 1960-1980
Here is a timeline of computer technology from 1960 - 1980:

1960 - The first PDP computer was introduced by Digital Equipment Corporation. It was the first mini-computer and marked the starting point of the mini-computer era.

1964 - System/360 mainframe was unveiled by IBM. This system allowed businesses to upgrade their systems without having to completely replace their hardware.

1965 - Gordon Moore, the co-founder of Intel, first introduced "Moore's Law," predicting that the number of transistors that could be placed on an integrated circuit would double approximately every two years.

1969 - The first message between two computers was sent over ARPANET, a predecessor to the Internet.

1970 - The first floppy disk was introduced by IBM, providing an easier way to store and transfer data.

1971 - Intel introduced the first microprocessor, the Intel 4004.

1973 - The first graphical user interface (GUI) was developed at the Xerox Palo Alto Research Center.

1975 - The first personal computer kit, the Altair 8800, was introduced by Micro Instrumentation and Telemetry Systems (MITS).

1976 - Apple Computer, Inc. was founded by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne.

1977 - The Commodore PET, the Apple II, and the TRS-80 were all released, helping to kick start the home computer revolution.

1980 - Microsoft released its first version of the Microsoft Disk Operating System (MS-DOS).
