Suppose you are managing a marketing campaign. What are some methods you can use to measure the success of the campaign?
There are various methods that can be used to measure the success of a marketing campaign. These methods include:

1. Return on Investment (ROI): This measures the revenue generated by the campaign against the cost of the campaign to see if it was worth the investment.

2. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC): This measures how much it costs to acquire a new customer through the campaign.

3. Conversion Rate: This measures the percentage of people who took the desired action after interacting with the campaign, such as making a purchase or filling out a lead form.

4. Engagement Rates: This measures how well the campaign engaged with the audience, by looking at metrics like likes, shares, comments, and click-through rates.

5. Brand Awareness: This measures the increase in brand recognition and recall associated with the campaign.

6. Customer Lifetime Value (CLV): This measures the long-term value of customers acquired through the campaign.

7. Sales Growth: This measures the increase in sales directly attributable to the campaign.
