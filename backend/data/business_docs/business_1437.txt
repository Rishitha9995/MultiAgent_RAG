How many countries are allowed to join the European Union?
Currently, there is no fixed limit to the number of countries that can potentially join the European Union. Any European country that meets the criteria for EU membership, as set out by the Copenhagen Criteria in 1993, can apply to become a member of the EU. These criteria include having stable institutions that guarantee democracy, the rule of law, human rights, respect for and protection of minorities, and having a functioning market economy capable of coping with competitive pressure and market forces within the Union. Additionally, a prospective member must accept and adopt the entire body of EU law and regulations, known as the acquis communautaire. Ultimately, the decision to accept new members is made by the existing member states on the basis of a unanimous agreement.
