Generate a correct data model for a customer relationship management system
Here is a potential data model for a customer relationship management (CRM) system:

- **Customers:** This table would store information about customers, including information such as their name, contact information, address, and any other relevant information. Primary key would be a customer ID.
   * Columns: Customer ID, Name, Email, Phone Number, Address, etc.

- **Sales:** This table would store information about sales transactions, including customer ID, date of sale, total value of the sale, and any other relevant information. Primary key could be a sales ID.
   * Columns: Sales ID, Customer ID, Date of Sale, Total Sale Value, etc.

- **Products:** This table would store information about products or services offered, including product name, description, price, and any other relevant information. Primary key would be a product ID.
   * Columns: Product ID, Product Name, Description, Price, etc.

- **Interactions:** This table would store information about interactions between the company and the customer, including date of interaction, type of interaction (e.g. phone call, email), and any notes about the interaction. Primary key could be an interaction ID.
   * Columns: Interaction ID, Customer ID, Date of Interaction, Type of Interaction, Notes, etc.

- **Sales_Products:** This table would be used for keeping track of the products or services involved in each sale (since there may be multiple products or services per sale). It would contain the sales ID, product ID, and quantity sold. Primary key could be a combination of the Sales ID and the Product ID.
   * Columns: Sales ID, Product ID, Quantity Sold

This is just one potential data model for a CRM system. The actual data model used would depend on the specific needs and requirements of the company using the system.
