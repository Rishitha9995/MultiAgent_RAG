Given the two passages, compare and contrast their concepts.
Agile methodology and Waterfall methodology both are approaches to project management, but they vary in their concepts and manner of execution. Agile methodology is centered around rapid iterations, where changes and feedbacks are quickly integrated into the project. As such, the focus on this methodology is being able to respond to changes swiftly and effectively. On the other hand, Waterfall methodology follows a more sequential and linear approach. This means that the project progresses from one phase to the next in a structured manner, with each phase dependent on the successful completion of the previous one. While Agile methodology is more adaptive to change, Waterfall methodology is action-oriented and follows a set sequence of stages from beginning to end.
