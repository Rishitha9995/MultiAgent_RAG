Create a list of competencies necessary for a data scientist.
A data scientist must possess a combination of skills and competencies across different domains, including but not limited to:

1. Advanced knowledge of mathematics and statistics: A strong understanding of statistics is vital for cleaning and analyzing data, testing hypotheses, and interpreting results.

2. Proficiency in programming languages: A data scientist must be proficient in languages such as R, Python, SQL, and SAS, for managing and analyzing large datasets, building models and visualizations.

3. Machine learning: An understanding of machine learning techniques such as regression, classification, clustering, and neural networks is crucial for building predictive models.

4. Data manipulation and cleaning: A data scientist must know how to manipulate and clean data to derive insights and ensure data quality.

5. Data visualization skills: The ability to clearly and effectively communicate complex data insights through visual presentations is essential for data scientists.

6. Big data platforms and tools: Familiarity with big data platforms like Hadoop and Spark, as well as cloud computing, can help data scientists manage and analyze large datasets.

7. Business acumen: A data scientist must have an understanding of the industry and the business problem at hand, and be able to translate data insights into actionable business decisions.

8. Communication and collaboration: The ability to communicate effectively with both technical and non-technical stakeholders, and to work collaboratively as part of a team is critical for data scientists.

9. Problem-solving and critical thinking: A data scientist must possess strong problem-solving abilities and a critical thinking mindset to tackle complex, real-world data problems.

10. Continuous learning: The field of data science is constantly evolving, and a good data scientist must be willing to continuously learn and stay up to date with new techniques, technologies, and best practices.
