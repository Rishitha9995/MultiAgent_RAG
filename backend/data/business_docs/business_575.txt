How would you involve data visualization in product development?
Data visualization can be incredibly useful during the product development process. Here are some ways it can be used:

1. Understanding customer needs: Visualizing data from customer feedback or market research can help the development team better understand the needs and preferences of their target customers. For example, heat maps, pie charts or bar graphs can be used to represent data about customer's pain points, likes, dislikes, and demographic information.

2. Identifying trends and patterns: Visualizing large data sets can help product developers quickly identify trends and patterns that they may have otherwise missed. This can include things like seasonality, geographic preferences, or changes in customer behavior. Line graphs, scatter plots or area charts are useful for identifying trends and patterns.

3. Tracking progress: Data visualization can be used to represent progress within the product development process. For example, Gantt charts or other project management visualizations can help the team understand how they’re tracking against key milestones, and where any bottlenecks may be occurring.

4. Presenting information to stakeholders: Using data visualizations to communicate key metrics and progress updates to stakeholders can be more effective than using text-based reports. For example, a dashboard with real-time updates and interactive charts can help executives see the most important information at a glance and make decisions quickly.

Overall, data visualization can play a vital role in product development by helping the team better understand customer needs, identify trends, track progress, and communicate effectively with stakeholders.
