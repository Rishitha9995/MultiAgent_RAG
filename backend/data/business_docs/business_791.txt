Describe a conflict between two characters.
Once upon a time there were two good friends, Jack and Mark. They had been friends since childhood and cherished each other's company. One day, Jack and Mark got into a conflict over a business deal. Jack wanted to invest in a promising new venture while Mark was skeptical and thought it was too risky.

The conflict escalated quickly and soon turned into a heated argument. Jack was determined to pursue the opportunity, while Mark was resolute in his belief that it was a grave mistake. They both had their reasons for their stance, and neither was willing to back down.

Tempers flared and the two friends found themselves caught up in a vicious cycle of anger and resentment. Each one accused the other of being stubborn and shortsighted, and their friendship suffered as a result.

They stopped talking to each other for weeks, unable to find common ground. The tension between them was palpable, and it seemed as though their once-strong friendship was on the brink of falling apart.

After some time, however, Jack and Mark realized that their friendship was more important to them than any business deal. They eventually reconciled, coming to a compromise that satisfied both of them.

In the end, the conflict served as a valuable lesson for both Jack and Mark, reminding them of the importance of communication and understanding in any relationship.
