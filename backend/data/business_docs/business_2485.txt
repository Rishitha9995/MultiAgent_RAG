Provide two solutions to the following money management problem
There are two potential solutions to this money management problem:

Solution 1: Reducing expenses
The family should take a close look at their monthly expenses to identify places where they can cut back. 
Some recommended steps they could take include:
- Creating a budget: This would help them see where their money is going and prioritize their spending. 
- Eliminating non-essential expenses: This is where the family can cut back on the less important things. For example, they could stop eating out, cancel subscriptions/memberships, reduce their entertainment expenses, etc.
- Reducing essential expenses: This is where the family can reduce the cost of essential items such as housing (renting a smaller place), transportation (using public transport instead of driving), food (avoiding brand names), utilities (conserving energy), etc.

By implementing these expense reduction strategies, the family could save $2,000 or more each month to close the gap between their income and spending.

Solution 2: Increasing income
In addition to reducing expenses, another solution to this money management problem is to increase income. 
Some possible ways the family could do this include:
- Asking for a raise or working overtime at their current place of employment. 
- Taking on a side job: The family members could do freelance or part-time work, such as driving for Uber/Lyft or renting out a spare room on Airbnb.
- Selling belongings: The family could sell items they no longer need/use, through platforms such as eBay, Facebook Marketplace, or Craigslist.

By using one or several of these methods to increase their monthly income, the family could make an additional $2,000 or more each month, bringing their income to match their spending.
