Generate a short story with a climax and resolution.
Once upon a time, there lived a poor farmer named Jack who had a small piece of land. Jack had been tilling his land for years, but no matter what he did, the crops would not grow, and he remained poor. One day, as he was walking in the forest, he came across a beautiful golden tree. He remembered hearing a story from his grandmother that if he made a wish while touching the tree, it would come true.

Filled with hope, Jack walked towards the tree and wished for his crops to grow abundantly. Suddenly, the tree started shaking, and the leaves rustled. Jack heard a voice that said, "Your wish will be granted, but only if you promise to share your wealth with those in need." Jack readily agreed, and as soon as he reached home, he saw that his crops had sprung to life, and his fields were overflowing with grains.

Jack sold his crops in the market and became rich overnight. He built a huge mansion, bought new clothes, and ate rich food. But he never forgot his promise. Every month he would go into the village and distribute food and money to those in need. His wealth grew, and so did his generosity.

But one day, a huge storm came, destroying Jack's mansion and fields. Jack was back to square one, with nothing to his name. He was devastated, but he did not lose hope. He went back to the golden tree and made the same wish again. The tree shook, and the voice said, "Your wish will be granted again, as you kept your promise and shared your wealth."

Jack began to rebuild his life. This time, he not only helped those in need but also taught them how to farm and make a living. He created employment opportunities and enhanced the lives of all those around him. Jack was never richer, and the village flourished.

The moral of the story is that true wealth is not measured by how much you have but by how much you give. Jack's generous heart brought him the greatest prosperity, and the golden tree was just a catalyst.
