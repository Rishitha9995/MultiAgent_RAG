How can we solve the problem of global poverty?
Solving global poverty is a complex challenge and requires a multi-faceted approach involving cooperation across borders, governments, non-profits, and the private sector. Some potential solutions include:

1. Increasing foreign aid and development assistance- Governments and international organizations should increase their aid towards poverty-stricken countries to finance programs that will provide the basis for sustained economic growth.

2. Promoting economic growth through free trade- This involves reducing trade barriers so that developing countries can have access to larger markets which can, in turn, drive economic growth.

3. Empowering local communities and giving them the tools they need to become self-sufficient- For example, by providing education, training, and access to financial services, individuals and communities can become less dependent on outside assistance.

4. Improving infrastructure- Lack of adequate infrastructure such as roads, healthcare facilities, utilities, and schools can inhibit a country's growth. Investments in infrastructure projects can help improve living standards and elevate people out of poverty.

Ultimately, poverty reduction is a long-term, ongoing effort that requires the cooperation of multiple parties. By emphasizing sustainable development, good governance, collaboration, and the engagement of local communities, it is possible to make progress towards the goal of ending global poverty.
