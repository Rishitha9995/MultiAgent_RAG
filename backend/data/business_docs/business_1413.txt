Who are the major figures in the French Revolution?
The French Revolution, which lasted from 1789 to 1799, was a period of radical social and political upheaval in French history. Some major figures that played a significant role in this revolution include:

1. Maximilien Robespierre - He was a prominent leader of the revolution, and one of the most influential figures associated with the Reign of Terror.

2. King Louis XVI - He was the last King of France before the fall of the monarchy during the Revolution. Louis XVI was executed in 1793.

3. Napoleon Bonaparte - He was a French military and political leader who rose to prominence during the French Revolution and became the first Emperor of France.

4. Georges Danton - He was a leading figure in the early stages of the Revolution and the first president of the Committee of Public Safety.

5. Jean-Paul Marat - He was a journalist and politician, best known for his publication of L'Ami du Peuple, a newspaper that became a voice for the people during the Revolution.

6. Marie Antoinette - She was the last Queen of France before the Revolution, and was known for her lavish lifestyle and her role in the downfall of the monarchy.

7. Charlotte Corday - She was a Girondin sympathizer who assassinated Jean-Paul Marat in 1793.

8. Jacques Necker - He was the finance minister of Louis XVI, and his dismissal in 1789 was one of the main triggers of the Revolution.

9. Louis de Saint-Just - He was a radical leader during the Revolution and a close ally of Robespierre. He played a significant role in the Reign of Terror.

10. Emmanuel-Joseph Sieyès - He was a clergyman and political writer who was a key figure in the early stages of the Revolution. He is best known for his pamphlet "What is the Third Estate?" which helped to fuel the revolutionary fervor of the time.
