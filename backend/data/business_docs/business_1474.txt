What type of data does a credit card hold?
A credit card holds a variety of data, including but not limited to: 

1. Personal information: This includes your name and your credit card account number. 

2. Magnetic stripe: A black strip on the back of your credit card contains sensitive information about your account necessary to complete a transaction. This strip contains your account number, the expiration date, and the credit card's security code.

3. Chip: Many modern credit cards contain a small metallic chip as an added measure of security. The chip generates a unique transaction code each time the card is used, reducing the risk of fraudulent transactions. 

4. Card details: This includes the credit card's expiration date, which is printed on the front of the card, as well as the security code- also known as the card verification value (CVV), which is a 3 or 4 digit code printed on the back of the card. 

5. Hologram: Some credit cards feature a hologram as an added security measure to prevent counterfeiting. 

This information is used to verify your identity, authorize transactions, and prevent fraudulent activities. Additionally, your credit card company may also store data about your account activity, such as your purchase history, payment history, and credit limit.
