Give an example of a successful negotiation and explain why it was effective.
Here's one example of a successful negotiation that illustrates several effective tactics:

A man wanted to purchase a new car and went to the dealership with a specific model in mind. He had done his research on the fair market value of the car and knew that the sticker price was about $2,000 higher than the average sale price of the car. When he spoke to the salesman, he stated his desire for the car, but also that he was not willing to pay the list price. He also mentioned that he had another dealership nearby offering a similar car for lower.

The salesman was eager to make the sale and offered a $1,000 discount. The buyer countered, stating that he could pay only $500 above the fair market value he had researched, and otherwise, he'd have to continue his search. The salesman then spoke with his manager to see if they could provide any additional discounts.

In the meantime, the buyer mentioned that he was interested in an extended warranty and potentially some other add-ons. The end result was that the dealership agreed to sell the car to the buyer for $700 below the list price, and the buyer agreed to purchase the extended warranty and few other add-ons.

This negotiation was effective because:

- The buyer was well-informed about the market price of the car and was able to use that information to his advantage.

- The buyer showed that he was willing to walk away from the deal and had other options. This created pressure on the dealership to try and close the sale with him.

- Each side was willing to make concessions, and both the buyer and the dealership showed some flexibility in their positions.

- The buyer introduced new elements to the negotiation, such as the extended warranty, which allowed for more room for compromise and helped both sides feel like they were getting something they wanted.
