It is unethical for a company to use facial recognition technology to create psychological profiles of its customers for several reasons, including:

1. Invasion of Privacy: An individual's psychological profile represents very intimate and personal information. Collecting this data without explicit permission is a severe invasion of their privacy.

2. Lack of Consent: Creating a psychological profile of someone is a complex process that should only be carried out with their explicit consent. Doing so without their knowledge and permission is an unethical practice.

3. Potential Misuse of Data: The data collected through facial recognition and the subsequent psychological profiles created can be easily misused or abused. This sensitive information could be used to manipulate or discriminate against individuals.

4. Inaccuracy: Psychological profiling through facial recognition technology is not necessarily accurate, and there is a risk of falsely labeling or misjudging individuals based on incorrect information, which can have severe consequences for them.
