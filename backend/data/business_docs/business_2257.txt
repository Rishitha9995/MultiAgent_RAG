Name five ways to improve air quality
1. Planting trees: Trees are natural air filters that remove pollutants by absorbing them through their leaves, bark and roots. Additionally, trees release oxygen which improves overall air quality.

2. Use public transportation or carpool: Fewer cars on the road means less air pollution. When people use public transportation or carpool, it helps lower the amount of emissions from vehicles which improves air quality.

3. Conserve energy: Conserving energy reduces the demand for energy generation, which in turn reduces the amount of fossil fuels burned, reducing air pollution. Turn off lights and appliances when not in use, and opt for energy-efficient gadgets and appliances.

4. Proper waste disposal: Burning of waste contributes heavily to air pollution. Dispose waste properly through recycling, composting, or following the waste management practices in your community.

5. Stay informed and support policies: Support legislation and government initiatives aimed at improving air quality such as emission standards for industries and vehicles, promotion of clean energy, and protection of natural resources. Stay informed about the air quality in your area and take necessary precautions such as avoiding outdoor activities when there is an air quality alert.
