What are the different roles of a bank?
A bank is a financial institution that serves a variety of roles within the economy. Some of the different roles of a bank include:

1. Accepting Deposits: One of the primary roles of a bank is to accept deposits from customers and hold their money in secure accounts.

2. Providing Loans: Banks loan money to individuals and businesses to help them finance large purchases or investments.

3. Facilitating Transactions: Banks act as intermediaries between buyers and sellers by facilitating transactions, such as transferring funds between accounts or issuing checks.

4. Investment: Banks also invest the money they hold in various assets, such as stocks, bonds, and real estate, in order to generate a profit.

5. Risk Management: Banks must carefully manage risk in order to remain profitable, by assessing the creditworthiness of borrowers and setting interest rates accordingly.

6. Financial Advice: Many banks provide financial advice to their customers to help them make informed decisions about investing, saving, and managing their money.

7. Currency Exchange: Banks often provide currency exchange services, both as a convenience for their customers and as a means of generating income.

Overall, banks play a crucial role in the financial system by providing stability, liquidity, and security to the economy.
