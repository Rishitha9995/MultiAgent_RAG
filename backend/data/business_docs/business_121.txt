What could result from the following business decision?
If a business decides to increase the price of a product by 10%, several outcomes could result from this decision. Here are some possibilities:

1. Increased revenue: If the demand for the product is relatively inelastic, meaning that the quantity demanded by customers does not change significantly with a price change, then the business could see an increase in revenue.

2. Loss of customers: If the demand for the product is elastic and highly sensitive to price changes, increasing the price could result in the loss of customers, who may choose to switch to cheaper alternatives.

3. Improved brand image: If the product is positioned as a premium or luxury item, increasing the price could enhance the brand image and make it more attractive to high-end consumers. 

4. Greater profit margins: If the increase in price is not accompanied by an increase in production costs, the business could enjoy higher profit margins.

5. Changes in market competition: If competitors do not follow suit with the price increase, it could result in a shift in market share. However, if competitors respond with similar price increases, the overall market dynamic may remain unchanged.

Ultimately, the outcome of increasing the price of a product by 10% would depend on various factors such as the price elasticity of demand, the competitive landscape, and the positioning of the product.
