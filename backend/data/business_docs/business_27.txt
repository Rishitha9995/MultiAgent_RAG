What is the purpose of Financial Accounting?
The primary purpose of financial accounting is to provide accurate, relevant, and reliable financial information to external users, such as investors, creditors, and regulators, to help them evaluate the financial performance and position of a company. Through financial accounting, companies record, summarize and present financial data in the form of financial statements, including the income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement. This information allows users to make informed decisions about investment, lending, and regulatory compliance. Financial accounting follows generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP) and is subject to audits and independent verification to ensure its accuracy and usefulness.
