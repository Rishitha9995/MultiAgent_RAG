Provide three facts about Poland.
1. Poland is a country located in Central Europe and has a population of around 38 million people. Its official language is Polish, and its capital and largest city is Warsaw.
2. Poland has a rich history and has played a key role in many important events such as World War II, the Solidarity movement, and the fall of communism in Eastern Europe. The country has a vibrant culture, with notable contributions in the fields of music, literature, film, and art.
3. Poland is a member of the European Union, NATO, the United Nations, and the World Trade Organization, among other international organizations. Its economy is considered to be one of the fastest-growing in the EU, with major industries including agriculture, manufacturing, and service industries such as finance, information technology, and tourism.
