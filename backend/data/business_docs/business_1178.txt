Tell a story that explains the concept of karma.
Once upon a time in a far-off land, there lived a poor farmer named Rohan. He lived a simple life with his family in a small village. Despite the daily struggles and hardships, Rohan was always kind and helpful to others around him. He would often bring food to his ill neighbor or help other farmers in the village with their work.

One day, while he was walking through the woods, Rohan came across an old woman who seemed lost. She asked for his help, and without hesitation, Rohan decided to help her find her way back home. The old woman was grateful and thanked him before she left. As Rohan continued walking, he stumbled upon a small pouch filled with gold coins. Overjoyed, he brought the pouch back home and shared his wealth with his family and villagers.

On the other hand, there was a rich merchant named Thomas, who lived in the same village. Thomas was not a kind man, and he would often mistreat others and look down upon them. One day, while he was riding through the village, a poor beggar approached him, asking for some food. Thomas, in his arrogance, refused to help the beggar and even kicked him aside.

The following day, while Thomas was traveling to another town for business, his cart's wheel broke, and he was stranded on the road. He had no water or food, and no one was willing to help him. Eventually, he had to walk back to the village, tired and hungry. When he reached home, he found out that his wealth had been stolen.

Karma is a concept that teaches us that our actions have consequences. When we do good deeds, we create positive energy that comes back to us in the form of good fortune or blessings. On the other hand, when we do harm or wrong to others, the negative energy we create will eventually find its way back to us in the form of misfortune or suffering. Just like Rohan's kindness was rewarded, and Thomas's actions led to his downfall, our actions shape our future.
