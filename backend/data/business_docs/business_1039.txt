How would you protect a garden from rabbits?
Rabbits are cute but they can wreak havoc on a garden. Here are some steps you can take to protect your garden from rabbits:

1. Install fencing: The most effective way to keep rabbits out of a garden is by installing a fence. Use a wire mesh fence that is at least two feet high and buried at least six inches underground to prevent rabbits from digging under it.

2. Use repellents: There are many different rabbit repellents on the market that can help to deter rabbits from coming into your garden. These can be in the form of sprays, granules or ultrasonic devices. Be sure to follow the instructions carefully and reapply as needed.

3. Plant deterrents: Some plants, such as onions, garlic, lavender, and catnip, are known to repel rabbits. Planting these around the perimeter of your garden can help to keep rabbits away.

4. Eliminate hiding places: Rabbits like to hide in overgrown vegetation and brush piles. Keep your garden tidy and free of debris to make it less attractive to rabbits.

5. Use traps: If all else fails, you can use humane traps to capture and relocate rabbits. Be sure to check with your local authorities before taking this approach, as there may be laws and regulations governing the trapping and relocation of wildlife.
