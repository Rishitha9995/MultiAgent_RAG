Construct a timeline of the events leading up to the American Revolution.
Here is a timeline of some of the major events leading up to the American Revolution:

1754-1763 - French and Indian War (also known as the Seven Years War): This war between the British and the French, with Native American allies on both sides, took place in North America and resulted in a British victory. However, the war was costly and led to the British government seeking to recover expenses through increased taxation of its North American colonies.

1764 - Sugar Act: The Sugar Act was passed by the British government to raise revenue from its North American colonies by taxing imports of molasses and sugar.

1765 - Stamp Act: The Stamp Act, another tax imposed by the British government on its North American colonies, required that all printed materials, including newspapers, legal documents, and playing cards, carry a stamp showing that a tax had been paid.

1765 - Quartering Act: The Quartering Act required the colonies to provide housing and supplies for British troops.

1766 - Repeal of the Stamp Act: After widespread protests and a boycott of British goods, the Stamp Act was repealed, but the British government also passed the Declaratory Act, which asserted their right to pass laws for the colonies.

1767 - Townshend Acts: The British government passed the Townshend Acts, which placed a tax on certain imported goods such as glass, paint, paper and tea.

1770 - Boston Massacre: In Boston, tensions between the British soldiers and colonists led to a violent confrontation in which five colonists were killed.

1773 - Boston Tea Party: In response to the Tea Act, which gave the British East India Company a monopoly over the tea trade in the colonies, a group of colonists in Boston threw a shipload of tea into the harbor.

1774 - Intolerable Acts: In response to the Boston Tea Party, the British government passed the Intolerable Acts, also known as the Coercive Acts, a series of measures designed to punish Massachusetts and restore order in the colonies. These measures included closing the port of Boston, limiting the power of town meetings, and revoking the Massachusetts Charter.
