Compile a timeline of the important events of the 20th century.
Here are some important events of the 20th century:

1903- Wilbur and Orville Wright made their first successful flight in an airplane in Kitty Hawk, North Carolina
1914 to 1918- World War I: One of the deadliest conflicts in history, claiming millions of lives.
1917- Russian Revolution: the overthrow of the Tsarist autocracy and formation of Soviet Union.
1920- Women gain the right to vote in the United States through the 19th Amendment.
1929- The Wall Street Crash: a major stock market crash that set off the Great Depression, the worst economic crisis in Western history.
1939 to 1945- World War II: A global conflict that claimed the lives of over 60 million people, included the Holocaust in which millions of Jews were killed by the Nazis.
1945- The Atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki by US, proving the devastating power of nuclear weapons.
1947- Indian independence from the British Empire.
1950 to 1953- Korean War: Tensions rise between Communist and non-Communist nations.
1954- Brown v. Board of Education decision: the US Supreme Court ended racial segregation in schools.
1962- The Cuban Missile Crisis: where tensions between US and Soviets come close to triggering nuclear war.
1963- Martin Luther King Jr. delivers his "I Have a Dream" speech during the March on Washington.
1969- The first human Moon landing as part of the Apollo 11 mission by US.
1975- End of Vietnam War: Vietnam was reunited under Communist rule.
1985- Mikhail Gorbachev becomes leader of USSR, leading to major reforms.
1989- Fall of Berlin wall; signify the end of the Cold War and Soviet Union.
1990 to 1991- The Gulf War: coalition forces expelled Saddam Hussein’s Iraqi troops from Kuwait.
1991- The dissolution of the Soviet Union into 15 independent republics.
1994- Nelson Mandela becomes South Africa’s first black president.
1999- Introduction of Euro, the common currency of the European Union.
