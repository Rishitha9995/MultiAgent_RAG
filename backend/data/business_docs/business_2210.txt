Given the input, list the differences between an e-book and traditional book
There are several notable differences between an e-book and traditional book, including:

1. Format: The primary difference is that an e-book is a digital file, while a traditional book is a physical copy. E-books can be read on electronic devices such as Kindle, mobiles, PCs, and tablets, while traditional books are read in their paper form.

2. Access: E-books can be downloaded and read instantaneously from the internet, while traditional books must be purchased or borrowed from a library/bookstore.

3. Space: E-books occupy no physical space, allowing readers to store thousands of books on a single device. On the other hand, traditional books can take up a large amount of space in a home or library.

4. Convenience: E-books offer convenience in terms of portability, as well as adjustable font sizes and back-lit screens for easier reading. Traditional books, on the other hand, don't need batteries and can be read without accessories.

5. Cost: E-books are usually less expensive than physical books, and many classic titles are available for free in electronic form. However, a device to read them on can be expensive.

6. Shareability: Traditional books are easily shareable and can be lent to friends or sold to a used bookstore. However, it is generally not permitted to share e-books due to digital rights management (DRM) restrictions.

7. Environmental impact: E-books have virtually no environmental impact, while the production of traditional books involves the use of paper, ink, and energy sources for printing.

8.  Personal Preferences: Some people prefer the feel and smell of a traditional book. Alternatively, many people enjoy the customizable features that an e-book offers.
