Describe three potential benefits of good organizational structure.
1. Improved Efficiency : A good organizational structure helps to streamline operations and improve efficiency by clearly defining roles, responsibilities and lines of communication. By eliminating areas of overlap or confusion, companies can minimize wasted time and effort, and employees can better understand their duties and focus on achieving the company's objectives.

2. Enhanced Communication : Good organizational design promotes open and effective communication across all levels of the company. It creates an environment where information is shared freely and employees feel comfortable discussing ideas, concerns and feedback. This can lead to greater innovation, stronger working relationships and quicker problem-solving, all of which can benefit the company's bottom line.

3. Heightened Flexibility : A well-designed organizational structure can be flexible enough to adapt to changes in the business environment. By recognizing the changing needs of the company and being able to respond quickly, organizations can stay ahead of the competition and continue to meet the needs of their customers. A good structure can also facilitate the adoption of new processes and technologies, allowing the company to remain agile and competitive in a fast-moving business landscape.
