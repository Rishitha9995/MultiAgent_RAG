Create a timeline showing the development of the car industry in the United States.
Here’s a timeline that displays the development of the car industry in the United States:

1. **1896** - Brothers Charles and J. Frank Duryea formed the Duryea Motor Wagon Company, becoming the first American company to build gasoline automobiles.
2. **1899** - Oldsmobile became the second American company to begin manufacturing automobiles.
3. **1901-1908** - Many auto factories were established during this time, include the Cadillac Motor Company, Ford Motor Company, and General Motors.
4. **1908** - The first Model T Ford was produced in Detroit, launching an era of affordable automobiles for the masses.
5. **1913** - Henry Ford introduced the world’s first moving assembly line at his plant in Highland Park, Michigan, revolutionizing the car production process.
6. **1920s** - The period saw the increased presence of electric lights, front-wheel brakes, and closed bodies on automobiles.
7. **1930s** - The car industry experienced a downturn due to the Great Depression, but improvements such as hydraulic brakes and radio continued.
8. **1940s** - The US auto industry shifted to the production of military vehicles during WWII, temporarily halting the manufacture of civilian automobiles.
9. **1950s** - The US economy recovered, and car companies flourished. Automatic transmission and power steering became standard features.
10. **1970s** - Foreign competition intensified, especially from Japan, and resulted in a decline in the US car industry.
11. **1980s** - Restructuring and modernization of the US car plants were done, which improved quality and made them more competitive.
12. **1990s** - The SUV gained popularity with the US public due to low gasoline prices, with truck-based SUVs gaining a dominant share in the market.
13. **2000s** - The automotive industry adopted more advanced technology, including hybrid engines, electric cars, and autonomous driving systems.
14. **2020** - The emergence of COVID-19 adversely impacted the automotive industry, disrupting production and sales of automobiles. However, the trend towards electric cars and autonomous driving continues to grow.
