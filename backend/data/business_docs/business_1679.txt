Generate a list of sample hedge fund strategies.
1. Long/Short Equity: This strategy involves taking long positions in stocks that are expected to appreciate, while short selling stocks that are expected to decline. 

2. Market Neutral: This strategy aims to eliminate market risk by taking long and short positions that balance each other out. 

3. Global Macro: This strategy involves taking positions in various financial instruments and markets based on the analysis of global economic and political events. 

4. Event-Driven: This strategy seeks to take advantage of price movements generated by corporate events such as mergers, acquisitions, spin-offs, and bankruptcies. 

5. Multi-strategy: A hedge fund that employs multiple strategies to achieve returns. 

6. Convertible Arbitrage: Involves going long on a convertible security while simultaneously shorting the underlying stock in an attempt to profit from pricing inefficiencies. 

7. Managed Futures: This strategy focuses on trading futures contracts in various markets including commodities, currencies, and indices. 

8. Fixed Income Arbitrage: This strategy involves taking long and short positions in fixed income securities to exploit pricing inefficiencies. 

9. Emerging Market: Investing in securities of companies or governments in emerging markets that are expected to grow rapidly. 

10. Fund of Funds: This type of hedge fund invests in a diversified portfolio of other hedge funds with the goal of achieving a balance between risk and return.
