Write two differences between a dog and a cat.
1. Behavior: One of the main differences between cats and dogs is their behavior. Dogs are known to be social creatures that generally enjoy being around people and other dogs. They are pack animals and they thrive on companionship and affection. On the other hand, cats are more independent animals and often prefer to be alone. They are not as social as dogs but they do enjoy the company of their human family members or other feline friends.

2. Physical Characteristics: Dogs come in a wide range of breeds, sizes, and shapes, but typically, they have an average size greater than cats. Their physical characteristics are also different, dogs have a muzzle, and their ears vary depending on the breed, some having long droopy ears while others have pointy ears. Cats, on the other hand, are generally smaller in size and have more uniform physical characteristics. They have sharp claws which they use for climbing, hunting, or self-defense, and they also possess excellent balance and are very agile.
