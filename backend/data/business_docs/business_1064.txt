Identify the changes in buying behavior during the COVID-19 pandemic.
During the COVID-19 pandemic, there have been significant changes in consumer buying behavior. Some of these changes include:

1. Online Shopping: With the closure of physical stores and the need for social distancing, there has been a notable increase in online shopping as consumers look for ways to safely purchase essential items.

2. Bulk Purchasing: There has been an increase in bulk-buying behavior as consumers look to stockpile essential items such as toilet paper, hand sanitizer, canned goods and cleaning supplies to limit their exposure to public places.

3. Health and Safety Products: There has been a significant increase in demand for health and safety products such as masks, gloves, disinfectants, and hand sanitizers, as consumers look to protect themselves and their families from the virus.

4. Food and Beverage: With restaurants and cafes closed, there has been an increase in demand for food and beverage items that can be prepared at home, such as packaged foods, frozen meals and baking supplies.

5. Changing Priorities: Due to financial uncertainty and job loss, consumers have shifted their priorities towards essential items and are cutting back on non-essential purchases such as luxury goods, clothing and travel.

6. Supporting Local Businesses: There has been a conscious effort by consumers to support local businesses, especially small and independent stores, as these have been hit hardest by the pandemic.

Overall, the COVID-19 pandemic has brought about significant changes in consumer buying behavior, as people adapt to the new normal and prioritize their health and safety.
