Construct a timeline of the internet's history
Here is a timeline of the internet's history:

Late 1960s: The U.S. Department of Defense funded the development of an early version of the internet called ARPANET. It was designed to allow researchers to share information and resources.

1972: Email was introduced by Ray Tomlinson, who developed a system for sending messages between different computers on the same network.

1982: The term "Internet" was first used, and the standard communication protocol, TCP/IP, was adopted.

1983: The Domain Name System (DNS) was introduced, making it easier to connect to websites using an easily remembered address instead of an IP address.

1989: Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web, a system for sharing information using hypertext that could be accessed using a web browser.

1991: The first website was launched, and the World Wide Web became publicly available.

1993: The first search engine, called Archie, was introduced.

1994: The first e-commerce transaction took place when someone purchased a Sting CD on NetMarket.

1995: Companies like Amazon and eBay were launched, and online shopping became more popular.

1998: Google was launched, quickly becoming the most popular search engine.

2001: Wikipedia was launched, providing a collaborative platform for users to create and share knowledge.

2004: Social media platforms like Facebook were introduced, allowing people to connect with friends and family online.

2005: YouTube was created, making it easy for people to share and watch videos online.

2007: The iPhone was launched, revolutionizing the way people accessed the internet through mobile devices.

2010: Instagram was launched, becoming a popular platform for sharing photos and videos.

2020: The world relies on the internet more than ever, with millions of people working and communicating online during the COVID-19 pandemic.
