Create a timeline illustrating the history of a certain item.
- **1817**: The first bicycle, called the “running machine,” was created by Karl von Drais. This early version had no pedals and was propelled by the rider pushing their feet against the ground. 

- **1839**: A Scottish blacksmith, Kirkpatrick Macmillan, added pedals to the bicycle, allowing riders to propel themselves without touching the ground.

- **1867**: At the World Exhibition held in Paris, the first mass-produced bicycles were displayed to the public.

- **1870**: The first chain-driven bicycle was created. This allowed for more efficient use of pedal power.

- **1880**: John Dunlop, a Scottish veterinarian, invented the first practical pneumatic tire, drastically improving the ride quality of bicycles.

- **1888**: The safety bicycle was invented by J.K. Starley. With a chain drive, two equal-sized wheels, and a padded seat, this design remains the standard for bicycles today.

- **1890s**: The popularity of bicycles exploded, with bicycle clubs, races, and tours flourishing around the world.

- **1903**: The first Tour de France, a multi-stage bicycle race, was held. The event has been held annually ever since.

- **1970s**: BMX bikes, designed for off-road racing and stunt riding, became popular.

- **1980s**: Mountain biking gained popularity, with off-road trails and races becoming common.

- **1990s**: The first commercially successful electric bikes were produced.

- **Present**: Bicycles continue to evolve, with new materials, technologies, and designs emerging in the market. Bicycles are widely used for transportation, recreation, and sport.
