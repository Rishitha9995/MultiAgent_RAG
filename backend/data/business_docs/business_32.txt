List five factors that lead to global warming.
1. Greenhouse Gas Emissions: The burning of fossil fuels releases large amounts of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere, which creates a heat-trapping blanket around the Earth, leading to global warming.

2. Deforestation: The clearing of forests not only releases stored carbon into the atmosphere, but also reduces the amount of carbon dioxide that can be absorbed by trees through photosynthesis, exacerbating global warming.

3. Industrialization: The industrial processes, particularly in manufacturing, cement production, and mining, produce large amounts of greenhouse gases, which contribute significantly to global warming.

4. Agriculture: The farming industry releases methane, a potent greenhouse gas, through livestock digestion, manure management, rice cultivation, and the use of fertilizers.

5. Transportation: The transportation sector is a significant source of greenhouse gas emissions, particularly carbon dioxide, due to the burning of gasoline and diesel fuel in vehicles and airplanes.
