Explain the "golden circle" concept in business
The Golden Circle is a concept in business popularized by Simon Sinek in his TED Talk, “Start with Why.” It is a model that describes how inspirational leaders and successful organizations can communicate and operate more effectively. The concept is represented by three concentric circles in the following order from outside to inside: ‘What’, ‘How’, and ‘Why’.

The outermost circle represents ‘What’ and it refers to an organization’s products or services-what the company does. This is the most straightforward and tangible aspect of the business, and it is how most companies present themselves.

The middle circle represents ‘How’, and it refers to the processes, values, or unique selling propositions that distinguish one company from another- how the company does what it does.

The innermost circle represents ‘Why’, and it refers to the company’s core purpose, belief, or cause-the reason why the company was founded and the reason it exists.

Sinek argues that companies who focus on their ‘Why’ first, followed by ‘How’ and then ‘What’ in their communications and actions, will be more successful and inspirational than those who focus only on ‘What’ they do. This is because when companies operate from their ‘Why,’ they are able to connect with customers on a deeper, emotional level, which leads to greater loyalty and success.
