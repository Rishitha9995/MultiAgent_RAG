Describe the difference between an artist and an illustrator.
An artist and an illustrator both use creative methods to communicate ideas and emotions through visual images. However, the main difference between them lies in the purpose of their work.

An artist creates works of art that are original expressions of their personal vision or interpretation of the world. They are often driven by an inner need to create something meaningful and to express their emotions and ideas through their medium of choice. Their work is usually intended for exhibition or for sale, and they have the freedom to explore their own ideas without being limited by commercial considerations.

An illustrator, on the other hand, creates images that are meant to accompany text or to serve a specific purpose in a commercial context. They work closely with art directors, authors, or clients, creating images that help convey a message or tell a story. Their work is often published in books, magazines, advertisements, or used in products or packaging, and must meet the specific needs and requirements of the client.

In summary, the main difference between an artist and an illustrator lies in the intention and purpose behind their work. While an artist creates work as an expression of their own vision, an illustrator creates work to serve a specific purpose or to communicate a particular message.
