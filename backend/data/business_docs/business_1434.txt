Create a rule for settling disputes between two senior managers.
When disputes arise between two senior managers, it is important that the situation is handled promptly and fairly. Here is a rule that can be followed to settle disputes between them:

1. Schedule a formal meeting: Bring both managers together for a meeting dedicated to addressing the conflict. 

2. Define the problem: Begin the meeting by clearly defining the issue at hand. Each manager should have an opportunity to present their perspective on the situation.

3. Explore solutions: Encourage the managers to brainstorm potential solutions to the conflict. Keep the discussions focused and ensure that each manager has equal opportunity to provide their input.

4. Agree on a course of action: Once all possible solutions have been presented, the managers should come to a mutual agreement on the best course of action. Ensure that the agreed-upon solution is fair and practical for both parties.

5. Monitor progress: Once the conflict has been resolved, it is important to monitor the situation to ensure that the agreed-upon solution is effective and that further disputes do not arise.

6. Escalate if necessary: If the managers are unable to come to an agreement or if the issue escalates, the situation should be escalated to a higher authority, such as the HR department or a company executive, for further guidance and mediation.

By following this rule for settling disputes, conflicts between senior managers can be resolved promptly and fairly, promoting a positive work environment and effective communication between team members.
