Describe the features of capitalism.
Capitalism is an economic system characterized by private ownership of the means of production and the creation of goods and services for profit. The key features of capitalism include:

1. Free-market economy: Under capitalism, markets are generally free from government intervention, and economic decisions are primarily driven by the laws of supply and demand. Prices are determined by consumer demand and competition among producers.

2. Private property rights: In a capitalist system, individuals and businesses have the right to own and control property, including the means of production. This allows them to invest, produce, and distribute goods and services for profit.

3. Competition: Capitalism is characterized by competition between businesses, which drives innovation and efficiency. This competition can help to lower prices for consumers and improve the quality of goods and services.

4. Consumer sovereignty: In a capitalist economy, consumers have a significant degree of control over what is produced as their demand for goods and services directs what goods and services are produced and in what quantities.

5. Profit motive: Capitalism is driven by the pursuit of profit. Businesses strive to maximize profits by increasing efficiency, reducing costs, and investing in new technologies and ventures. Profits provide an incentive for businesses to take risks, innovate, and expand.

6. Limited government intervention: While the government has a role in regulating markets and enforcing laws, there is generally limited intervention in capitalist economies. Government intervention typically focuses on creating a positive business environment, protecting property rights, and maintaining a stable currency.
