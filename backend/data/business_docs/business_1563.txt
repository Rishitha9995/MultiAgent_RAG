Give an example of a data mining problem that consists of open-ended generation.
One example of a data mining problem that consists of open-ended generation is the generation of new and creative product ideas or features based on customer data analysis. This can involve analyzing customer feedback and reviews, purchasing habits, and other relevant data to identify patterns and trends. Based on this analysis, a company can generate new ideas for products or features that are likely to be well-received by their customers. Unlike other data mining tasks that focus on predicting or classifying data, this problem involves the open-ended task of generating original ideas, making it more complex and challenging.
