Tell me what the primary purposes of a company website are.
The primary purposes of a company website are to:

1. **Provide information** about the company, its products, and services: A company website is a platform for providing customers with all the information they require about the company – its history, vision, and mission, what it does, products or services it offers, new product launches, and so on.

2. **Attract new customers**: By having an online presence, companies can reach out to a global audience and attract new customers. For example, a user searching for a particular product or service may come across the company's website and become a new customer.

3. **Engage and interact with customers**: A website is also a platform through which companies can communicate with their customers, for example, through a contact form, live chat, or chatbot. This creates an avenue for customers to ask questions, provide feedback, and interact with the company.

4. **Sell products and services**: Many companies use their website as an e-commerce platform to sell products and services directly to customers.

5. **Build credibility and establish a reputation**: A professional-looking website helps to establish the company's credibility and reputation in the marketplace. It is an online representation of the company, and it should present the company's image and values positively.

6. **Support marketing efforts**: A website is essential to a company's digital marketing strategy. Search engine optimization (SEO), pay-per-click (PPC) advertising, and social media marketing all rely on the company's website to be effective.
