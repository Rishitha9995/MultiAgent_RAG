Summit a list of challenges faced by the retail industry
1. Intense competition: Retailers are facing growing competition from a growing number of domestic and international retailers across different retail categories.
2. Digitization and e-commerce: With the rise of online shopping, retailers have to establish an online presence and compete with pure-play e-commerce giants for market share.
3. Changing consumer behavior: With the emergence of digital technologies, consumer behavior has changed, and retailers have to adapt to these changes to remain competitive.
4. Inventory management: Retailers have to manage their inventory levels efficiently to avoid stock-outs, overstocking, and losses due to obsolescence.
5. Price pressure: Retailers are under pressure to keep prices low due to increased competition, which can lead to lower margins and profitability.
6. High operational costs: Running a retail store incurs high operational costs like rent, labor, utilities, and marketing, which can eat into the retailer's profits.
7. Supply chain disruptions: Retailers are vulnerable to supply chain disruptions due to natural disasters, strikes, and other unforeseen events, which can cause delays and stock-outs.
8. Technological advancements: Retailers have to keep up with the latest technological advancements in retail like automation, artificial intelligence, and data analytics to improve operational efficiency and enhance the customer experience.
9. Shifting demographics: Retailers have to cater to an increasingly diverse population with different needs, tastes, and preferences.
10. Regulatory changes: Retailers have to comply with a range of regulations like labor laws, data privacy laws, and environmental regulations, which can increase the cost of doing business.
