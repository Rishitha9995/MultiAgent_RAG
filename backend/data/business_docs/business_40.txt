Outline an itinerary for a three day trip.
Day 1:
- 9:00 am: Start your trip by visiting the famous Tower of London, where you can take a tour and see the Crown Jewels.
- 12:00 pm: Head to Borough Market for lunch, where you can sample local and international foods.
- 2:00 pm: Take a walk along the Thames River and cross the Tower Bridge to enjoy panoramic views of the city.
- 4:00 pm: Visit the Tate Modern, which houses contemporary and modern art from around the world.
- 7:00 pm: Have dinner at a traditional British pub, where you can try dishes like fish and chips or shepherd's pie.

Day 2:
- 9:00 am: Start your day by taking a tour of the Houses of Parliament and listen to a debate in the House of Commons or House of Lords.
- 12:00 pm: Have lunch at a cafe in Covent Garden, where you can also enjoy some shopping and street performers.
- 2:00 pm: Take a tour of Buckingham Palace, the residence of the Queen, and see the changing of the guard.
- 5:00 pm: Relax in one of London's famous parks, such as Hyde Park or St. James's Park.
- 7:00 pm: Have dinner in Soho, which has a vibrant nightlife and a wide range of restaurants to choose from.

Day 3:
- 9:00 am: Visit the British Museum, which has a vast collection of artifacts from around the world, including the Rosetta Stone.
- 12:00 pm: Have lunch in Camden Market, which has a bohemian vibe and a wide range of street food options.
- 2:00 pm: Take a tour of the Harry Potter studios, where you can learn about the making of the movies and see sets and costumes.
- 6:00 pm: Have dinner in Chinatown, which has a variety of Asian restaurants and is known for its lively atmosphere.
- 8:00 pm: End your trip by watching a show in the West End, London's theatre district.
