Give three examples of financial budgeting techniques.
1. Zero-based budgeting: This technique involves building a budget from scratch, where every expense has to be justified for each new period. It requires examining and reviewing all expenses and justifying their need before adding them to the budget. This approach is useful for cutting unnecessary costs and reallocating resources, but it requires a significant amount of time and effort.

2. The 50/30/20 Rule: This technique suggests dividing after-tax income into three categories: Needs, wants, and savings or debt repayment. In this method, 50% of the income is allocated to necessities such as housing, groceries and utilities, 30% to discretionary spending like entertainment and dining out, and the remaining 20% towards savings or debt repayment. This approach is helpful in setting spending priorities and managing finances efficiently.

3. Envelope System: This technique involves dividing cash into various envelopes labeled for each expense category. Once the money allocated to each category is spent, the envelope is emptied until the next time the budget is replenished. This approach helps people control their spending, avoid overspending, and stay within their budget.
